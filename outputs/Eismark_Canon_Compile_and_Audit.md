## Setting Pillars

Eismark is a dark fantasy/dieselpunk D&D setting built around memory, forgetting, sacrifice, truth, faith, identity, and the cost of knowledge.

Core cosmology:

- The Creator brings Creation into being.
- The First Ones are created, approximately fifteen in number.
- The First Ones create hundreds of lesser angels.
- The First Ones and lesser angels shape the world and its natural order.
- Mortal peoples inherit Creation.
- The Entity discovers Creation.
- The Veins open and the Blood enters reality.
- The Blood is not evil in the normal moral sense. It is incompatible with Creation and corrupts because reality cannot coexist with it.
- The Blood may appear as a liquid, sometimes flowing and sometimes not, with impossible mixing colors. Looking at it causes headaches and wrongness.
- Infection twists bodies and minds.
- No two bloodspawn are exactly the same.
- The Entity is curious rather than straightforwardly malicious.

## History Timeline

### Age of Creation

- HIS-001: Creation begins.
- HIS-002: The First Ones are created.
- HIS-003: Lesser angels are created.
- HIS-004: The world is shaped.
- Mortal peoples are created before the First Age fully blooms. See inconsistency I-001.

### First Age

- Mortal civilizations flourish under guidance from the First Ones and lesser angels.
- The Entity discovers Creation.
- The Veins rupture and the Blood enters Creation.

### The Long Vigil

- The First Ones, lesser angels, and mortals wage war against the Blood.
- The Blood cannot be permanently defeated through force.
- The angels sacrifice themselves by entering dormancy, many becoming statues.
- The Communion is created to seal the Veins.
- The Entity is prevented from fully entering Creation.

### Second Age / Lingering War

- The Veins are sealed, but Blood already inside Creation continues to ravage the world.
- Ancient Kaltheim becomes humanity's last great beacon.
- Ancient Kaltheim begins and completes the Joivian Project.
- The Joivians are holy war machines, most built around dormant lesser angels.
- Uriel's vessel is placed within one Joivian.
- Uriel contacts the Entity, learns forbidden truths, and willingly fractures to protect the Communion.
- Uriel's departure causes the Great Forgetting.
- The Joivians march into the Great Maw. None return.
- The remaining Blood is destroyed, contained, or driven back.
- The Lingering War ends.

### Age of Pilgrimage and Early Third Age

- Most of Ancient Kaltheim journeys north to seek the lost Joivians.
- After 153 years, the pilgrims found modern Kaltheim in the northern mountains.
- Those who remain behind rebuild the ruins of Ancient Kaltheim, eventually becoming Veloria.
- The Great Forgetting causes knowledge of the First and Second Ages to fade into myth.
- New civilizations emerge across Eismark.

### Third Age

- Modern Kaltheim consolidates as a mountain kingdom and successor to Ancient Kaltheim.
- Veloria consolidates among rebuilt Ancient Kaltheim ruins.
- Gn'ure emerges from older civilizations.
- The Sylvan Veil endures as an isolated ancient elven civilization.
- Dragonborn dominance over the Shattered Isles declines.
- Organized religion and great kingdoms become defining institutions.
- The Iron Church rises as the dominant religion across much of humanity.

## Nations and Identities

### Kaltheim

Identity: faith, duty, mountains, Iron Church, Consecrated, Exalted Engines, discipline, fortifications, industrial standardization.

Known geography/politics:

- Karhold is strongly implied by recovered chat prompts to be the capital of Kaltheim. Verify against map canon before locking.

Military identity:

- Formal procurement designations such as `MP-48`, `AC-42`, `BR-47`, `LMG-46`, `ATR-44`, `ATP-45`, `ASG-48`.
- No-nonsense industrial naming.
- Visual language: black iron, stamped steel, rivets, exposed bolts, vented shrouds, wire stocks, military diagrams, factory stamps, procurement tables.
- No wood on Kaltheim firearms.
- Exalted Engines are towering sacred walkers bearing scripture and religious iconography.
- Exalted Engines/mechs do not have pilots. They possess the same kind of consciousness as the Consecrated, but are larger and more specialized.
- They retain the intelligence and tactical understanding of the Consecrated.

Core armor doctrine:

- Field Gear: recon and support.
- Infantry Plate Rig: standard issue for most soldiers.
- Assault Harness: shock troops and breach teams.
- Heavy Weapons Harness: rare/specialized pressurized rig for flamethrowers, heavy weapons, rocket troops, and demolition engineers.
- Juggernaut Armor: elite platform with modular battlefield variants.

Core troop roster:

- Kaltheim Rifleman: `BR-47`.
- Kaltheim Assault Trooper: `ASG-48`.
- Kaltheim Machine Gunner: `LMG-46`.
- Kaltheim Combat Engineer: `AC-42`.
- Scout: `Longshot`, though this creates a faction-equipment question.
- Rocketeer: `ATP-45`.
- Flame Trooper: `FT-47`.
- Sniper: `ATR-44`.
- Storm, Dragon, Earthshaker, and Thunder Lance Juggernauts.

### Veloria

Identity: freedom, ambition, entrepreneurship, corruption, decentralized command, street-level initiative.

Military and weapon identity:

- Weapons use practical street names: Rotator, Slugger, Longshot, Rattler.
- Visual language: Art Deco borders, gold trim, jazz-age advertising, newspaper type, speakeasy and detective aesthetics.
- Materials: walnut, oak, polished brass, blued steel.
- Firearms should feel handcrafted, artisanal, old-fashioned, and culturally distinct from Kaltheim.

Important tension:

- One source says Velorians do not understand firearms engineering like Kaltheim does.
- Another ARP source says Veloria values craftsmanship, adaptation, and artisanal manufacturing.
- This is fixable if Veloria is defined as brilliant at practical gunsmithing and adaptation, but weaker at Kaltheim-style standardized industrial theory.

### Voss

Identity: nationalism and mechanized warfare. Doctrine specializes in armored breakthroughs.

### Rauk

Identity: industry, automatons, logistics, and attrition. Some recovered archive text spells this as `Ruak`.

Map note: the shared chat includes an instruction to switch Rauk/Ruak and the Free Marches on the map.

### Lan Cao

Identity: rationalism, science, magical artillery, artificers, and scientific warfare.

### Sylvan Veil

Identity: ancient elves, memory, isolation, and continuity through the Great Forgetting.

### Shattered Isles

Identity: Polynesian-inspired seafarers, dinosaurs, and Dragonborn homeland. Dragonborn dominance declines in the Third Age.

### Gn'ure

Identity: fallen Arthurian kingdoms and remnants of older civilizations.

## Campaign Frame

Recovered archive summary says the campaign begins in the Bar. The players have amnesia. Memories return on level-ups through rolls. The campaign revolves around recovering truth.

Known Bar-related elements from the archive summary:

## Continuity Issues and Fixes

### I-001: HIS-005 Has Two Different Meanings

Severity: High.

Conflict:

- `Eismark_Canon_Database_History_001-005.pdf` says HIS-005 is "The Mortal Races Are Created."
- `ECD_History_001-010_v0.1.pdf` and `ECD_Adjusted_History_Timeline_v1.pdf` say HIS-005 is "The First Age Begins."

Recommended ruling:

- Make HIS-005 "The Mortal Peoples Are Created."
- Make HIS-006 "The First Age Begins."
- Shift later history numbers forward, or create HIS-005A for mortal creation if renumbering is painful.

### I-002: Great Forgetting Timing Is Slightly Muddy

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- HIS-014 says Uriel's departure causes the Great Forgetting at the end of the Second Age.
- HIS-020 places "The Great Forgetting" in the Early Third Age.

Recommended ruling:

- Treat Uriel's fracture as the cause and beginning of the Great Forgetting.
- Treat HIS-020 as the historical/cultural completion point, when the loss becomes obvious across civilization.

### I-003: Joivian/Jovian Spelling

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- ECD files use `Joivian`.
- OCR from the archive image around Uriel reads `JOVIAN FORM`.

Recommended ruling:

- Use `Joivian` as canon.
- Treat `Jovian` as OCR error, old draft spelling, or in-world simplified/common misspelling.

### I-004: Rauk/Ruak Spelling

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- ARP files use `Rauk`.
- Chat archive page 475 says "switch ruak and the free marchs."

Recommended ruling:

- Use `Rauk` as canon unless a later map explicitly says otherwise.
- Mark `Ruak` as typo/old draft.
- Also verify the map instruction that Rauk and the Free Marches should be swapped geographically.

### I-005: "Iron Marches of Kaltheim" and "Free City of Veloria" May Be Deprecated

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- `Recovered chat part 1.txt` uses "The Iron Marches of Kaltheim" and "The Free City of Veloria."
- The archive OCR page 13 says to remove "the free city" and "the iron marches."

Recommended ruling:

- Do not use these as official nation titles.
- Keep them only as old supplement chapter headers, propaganda phrases, or deprecated development labels.

### I-006: Scout Uses Velorian Longshot in a Kaltheim Roster

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- The Kaltheim enemy roster gives the Scout "The Longshot," which is otherwise established as a Velorian weapon.

Recommended ruling:

- Either make the Scout a Velorian/recon auxiliary unit, or replace the weapon with a Kaltheim marksman/scout carbine designation.
- Suggested Kaltheim replacement: `SR-45 Scout Rifle` or `MR-46 Mountain Rifle`.

### I-007: Karhold Capital Needs Map Confirmation

Severity: Medium.

Issue:

- The shared chat includes the user correction: "isnt karhold the capital of Kaltheim."
- The accessible sources here do not include a full map key or nation gazetteer confirming the capital.

Recommended ruling:

- Tentatively mark Karhold as Kaltheim's capital.
- Confirm against the map reference or geopolitics document before publishing a gazetteer.

### I-008: Combat Engineer Weapon Was Corrected Mid-Development

Severity: Low.

Conflict:

- Earlier recovered text says Combat Engineer uses "Flamethrower or SMG."
- Later text explicitly says Engineer should have the `AC-42`; flamethrowers become specialist assault equipment.

Recommended ruling:

- Canon: Combat Engineer uses `AC-42`.
- Flame Trooper uses `FT-47` and Heavy Weapons Harness.

### I-009: Heavy Weapons Harness Stats Drift

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- Earlier armor list has Heavy Weapons Harness at AC 17, 65 lb, anti-tank role.
- Later standardized version has AC 16, 95 lb, combines Flame Suit and Heavy Weapons Harness, fire resistance, gas/blindness advantages, and load-frame features.
- ECD_ARP-003 says the Heavy Weapon Harness becomes a bulkier version of the Flame Suit.

Recommended ruling:

- Canon: later standardized Heavy Weapons Harness, AC 16, 95 lb, fireproof pressurized rig.
- Archive the earlier AC 17 version as a prototype or field variant.

### I-010: Juggernaut Armor AC Drift

Severity: Medium.

Conflict:

- Player/equipment version lists Juggernaut Armor as AC 20.
- Enemy base juggernaut armor later uses AC 18.

Recommended ruling:

- Separate them by use case:
  - Artifact/equipment Juggernaut Armor: AC 20, legendary, rare named gear.
  - Enemy Juggernaut platform: AC 18 for balance.
- Add an ECD/MIL note that enemy platform stats are encounter abstractions, not equipment rules.

### I-011: Velorian Engineering Identity Needs Clarification

Severity: Low.

Conflict:

- Source A: Velorians do not understand the engineering principles behind firearms the way Kaltheim does.
- Source B: Veloria values craftsmanship, adaptation, and artisanal manufacturing.

Recommended ruling:

- Veloria understands craft, repair, copying, modification, and practical performance.
- Kaltheim dominates theory, standardization, procurement, and mass manufacture.

### I-012: Entity Curiosity Versus "Inevitable" Vein Opening

Severity: Low.

Potential issue:

- Archive summary says the Entity is curious, not evil.
- Timeline says its discovery of Creation makes the opening of the Veins inevitable.

Recommended ruling:

- The Entity does not need malicious intent. Its attention alone stresses reality. The Veins open because Creation cannot bear contact with what exists outside it.

### I-013: Exalted Engines Need Their Own Category

Severity: Medium.

Issue:

- Some recovered material discusses mechs as military assets.
- The shared chat clarifies they are not piloted machines. They possess the same consciousness as the Consecrated and are closer to enormous specialized suits/war bodies.

Recommended ruling:

- Do not classify Exalted Engines as vehicles.
- Classify them as sacred war bodies or Consecrated-derived holy engines.
- In rules terms, they may use vehicle-like battlefield roles, but lore-wise they are conscious military beings.

### I-014: Bloodspawn Visual Canon Is Variable by Design

Severity: Low.

Issue:

- The user explicitly says no two bloodspawn are exactly the same.

Recommended ruling:

- Bestiary entries should define shared principles rather than a single fixed anatomy:
  - impossible Blood coloration,
  - body and mind distortion,
  - reality-incompatibility,
  - individualized mutations.

### I-015: Ancient Kaltheim as Last Beacon Appears Twice

Severity: Low.

Overlap:

- HIS-010 says Ancient Kaltheim becomes the last great beacon.
- HIS-011 repeats this as its own entry.

Recommended ruling:

- HIS-010 should establish the Lingering War and note civilization's collapse.
- HIS-011 should specifically detail Ancient Kaltheim's rise as the last bastion.

## ARP / Codex Workflow Notes
