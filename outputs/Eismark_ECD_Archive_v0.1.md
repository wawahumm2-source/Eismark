Earlier PDFs, recovered chats, images, and markdown exports are source material for this archive rather than equal canon authorities. Entries affected by active canon problems are marked `Under Review` and should be checked against `Eismark_Audit_Register_v0.3.md` before being locked as final canon. When this ECD archive conflicts with an older source document, the ECD archive takes precedence unless the issue is reopened in the Audit Register.

### HIS-016 - The Great Forgetting

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: The world loses much of its collective memory following Uriel's fracture and departure.

Canon Facts:

- Uriel's departure causes the Great Forgetting.
- The Great Forgetting begins immediately as a metaphysical phenomenon.
- Over generations, it becomes a civilizational collapse.
- Historians later refer to the entire era as The Great Forgetting.
- Knowledge of the First and Second Ages is largely lost.
- Many events pass into myth.
- The Great Forgetting did not erase civilization; it erased understanding.
- Languages, traditions, calendars, religions, holidays, and documents survived.
- What was lost was the ability to connect those inheritances into a coherent history.
- The people of the Third Age inherited a world full of answers without remembering the questions.
- People preserve names, holidays, calendars, and traditions long after forgetting their original meanings.
- Modern Kaltheimians say dates such as `1945 AV` without being able to answer what the Vigil was.
- `AV` means After Vigil.
- The meaning of the Vigil survives only in fragments.
- The Iron Church often teaches that the Vigil was Michael's eternal watch over mankind.
- Historians describe the Vigil as a prolonged period of conflict in antiquity.
- Ordinary citizens may say it was the time their ancestors protected civilization.
- Scholars often admit that they are not entirely sure.
- These different answers are not contradictions; they are inherited fragments.
- Modern Veloria uses `FC`, the Founding Compact, as a civil calendar and legal inheritance.
- Kaltheim measures time through religious history, while Veloria measures time through civil government.
- Neither civilization understands why it inherited these traditions, even when it understands how to use them.
- Even the First Ones possess incomplete memory because Uriel's fracture wounded the Communion.

Consequences:

- The Third Age begins with fragmented history.
- The campaign's memory mechanics echo this setting-wide wound.
- Ancient truths may survive as prayers, dates, legal formulas, oaths, holidays, place-names, and ceremonial language after their original meanings have been lost.
- A campaign reveal may occur when Uriel recognizes that Veloria's Founding Compact preserved memory of Ancient Kaltheim longer than anyone realized.

### HIS-018 - Calendrical Systems

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Following the Great Forgetting, different civilizations developed their own systems for reckoning time, each reflecting the values and inherited memory fragments of its culture.

Canon Facts:

- The major calendars of modern Eismark share a common historical origin in the aftermath of the Great Forgetting.
- Their underlying structure descends from ancient First Age calendrical practice, making them functionally analogous despite different year counts.
- Dates correspond roughly across nations, but are expressed differently depending on culture.
- After the Great Forgetting, civilizations continued measuring years but redefined Year One according to the event they believed mattered most.
- The calendars remain internally consistent while being historically incomplete.
- Kaltheim uses `AV`, After Vigil.
- Example Kaltheim date: `1945 AV`.
- The AV calendar commemorates the conclusion of the Long Vigil.
- Kaltheim's calendar reflects its identity as an ancient civilization defined by faith, sacrifice, and continuity.
- Kaltheim's history revolves around the Long Vigil, the Iron Church, the First Ones, and enduring against darkness.
- The original meaning of the Vigil has faded from public memory.
- Modern Kaltheimians can accurately date events in AV, but few can answer what the Vigil truly was.
- The Iron Church teaches that the Vigil was Michael's eternal watch over mankind.
- Historians describe the Vigil as an ancient period of prolonged conflict.
- Most citizens understand it as the era during which their ancestors protected civilization.
- Very few understand its true significance.
- Veloria uses `FC`, the Founding Compact.
- Example Velorian date: `1928 FC`.
- Veloria measures time from the ratification of the Founding Compact, the document that established the modern republic.
- Veloria deliberately rejects religious chronology.
- Veloria's calendar reflects a culture built around civic institutions, law, commerce, industry, optimism, and republican ideals.
- The Founding Compact remains one of Veloria's most treasured national relics.
- Its principles continue to shape Velorian law and civic identity.
- Portions of the document reference places, traditions, and historical events whose original meanings have been forgotten.
- Modern Velorians preserve the words faithfully without fully understanding the world that gave rise to them.

Historical Irony:

- Both calendars unknowingly commemorate consequences of the same forgotten age.
- Kaltheim remembers the end of the Long Vigil.
- Veloria remembers the birth of a new republic.
- Neither civilization realizes that both events ultimately trace their origins to the Great Forgetting and the collapse of the First Age.
- Kaltheim anchors time to faith, endurance, and the guardianship of Creation.
- Veloria anchors time to law, liberty, and the republic.
- This contrast helps define the identities of both nations.
- History was not only forgotten; it was reinterpreted.
- The past was not erased. Each civilization inherited fragments of it and built its identity around the pieces it happened to preserve.

Design Notes:

- Kaltheim and Veloria occupy the same historical world and roughly corresponding calendar moment, but they evoke different cultural eras.
- Kaltheim's current atmosphere draws from a cultural 1938-1945: militarized, ancient, sacrificial, fortified, and standing near the edge of catastrophic war.
- Veloria's current atmosphere draws from a cultural 1928: art deco skylines, jazz, electric lights, department stores, subways, stock exchanges, modern industry, and a future that still seems bright.
- These are aesthetic inspirations, not literal Earth equivalencies.
- The purpose is to let players feel the difference between the nations without reducing them to direct historical analogues.

### COS-005 - The Blood

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: The Blood is the physical manifestation of the Entity's corruption within Creation, an impossible substance that seeps from the wounded Veins of the world.

Canon Facts:

- It has no will, intelligence, or goals.
- It is not a natural substance.
- It is incompatible with Creation and exists outside the laws of reality.
- It is not always liquid and may flow when it should not or remain still when it should move.
- It displays impossible shifting colors that the mortal mind struggles to comprehend.
- It is not simply poisonous or magical; it is a force of corruption that rewrites reality itself.
- It corrupts flesh, mind, memory, soul, landscape, and reality.
- Rather than infecting victims, it rewrites them into something they were never meant to become.
- It causes sensory pain and an overwhelming feeling of wrongness.
- Its many hues are not separate substances, but different manifestations of the same cosmic corruption.
- Known manifestations are described poetically as bleeding without wounding, shining without light, moving without moving, whispering without voice, existing without reason, and not being meant to be seen.
- Exposure may leave victims partially aware, trapped inside changing forms, or erased entirely.
- Direct exposure to concentrated Blood may cause headaches, nausea, nosebleeds, dizziness, disturbing dreams, and pain from merely looking at it.
- Corruption often begins subtly, with the mind deteriorating before the body.
- Early mental corruption often appears as obsessive thoughts, intrusive dreams, personality shifts, dissociation, and emotional instability.
- Transformation is continuous and unstable.
- Blood corruption progresses through four observed stages: Whispering, Alteration, Fracture, and Blooming.
- Whispering causes dreams, compulsions, and intrusive thoughts.
- Alteration begins physical corruption as flesh changes, fingers lengthen, posture shifts, skin discolors, sensory abnormalities emerge, and the mind starts to unravel.
- Fracture brings pain, obsession, violence, loss of stable reality perception, and rapid changes to mind and body.
- Blooming is complete corruption, where the former self ceases to be recognizable and a Bloodspawn is born.
- When the Blood touches land, affected regions may exhibit unnatural silence, humming skies, fleeing wildlife, dying or twisted vegetation, distorted space and time, and a profound sense that reality has become fundamentally wrong.
- Witness phrases associated with Blood-tainted regions include "The woods were silent," "The ground was screaming," "The sky was humming," and "The hills were looking at us."
- Blood-tainted landscapes are best understood as reality rejecting an infection it was never meant to contain.
- Nothing that enters Blood-touched places leaves unchanged.
- The Blood is a cosmic force of unmaking that erases and overwrites Creation with something alien and impossible.
- The Blood does not create monsters in the ordinary sense; it creates failed attempts at something new.
- References to the Blood carrying a will to unmake Creation are poetic descriptions of the Entity's corrupting influence, not evidence that the Blood itself has independent agency.
- The Veins ceased producing new Blood after reality was sealed.
- Blood already present within Creation remained trapped there.
- Public knowledge of the Blood is fragmentary and confused; most people know only that a great darkness, plague, divine punishment, demon-war, or terrible age of war threatened civilization long ago.
- Most ordinary people have never heard the term "the Blood"; those who have usually assume it is a religious metaphor, old superstition, or allegory.
- The senior clergy of the Iron Church preserve the most complete surviving accounts, but even the Church lost much during the Great Forgetting.
- Uriel's Testament actively searches for evidence of the Blood, the First Age, the Lingering War, and the fate of the Joivians.
- The Dreamers of Azhul possess fragmentary and catastrophically misunderstood Blood texts.
- The Inquisition of the Vigil knows enough to treat any possible return of the Blood as a world-ending threat.

### REL-007 - The Number 153

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Among the faithful of the Iron Church, 153 is regarded as one of the holiest numbers in Kaltheimian tradition. It appears throughout religious life, though almost no one remembers why.

Canon Facts:

- The number 153 appears throughout Kaltheimian culture.
- It is common in monastery architecture, funerary rites, military ceremonies, pilgrimage traditions, illuminated manuscripts, rosaries, sacred measurements, and liturgical calendars.
- Its use is so widespread that few people ever question it.
- Monastic prayer cycles traditionally consist of 153 invocations.
- Pilgrims ascend 153 ceremonial steps before entering many great shrines.
- Memorial services often feature 153 candles.
- Bells ring 153 times during the highest days of remembrance.
- Consecrated rosaries traditionally contain 153 beads.
- Military memorials frequently incorporate the number into ceremonial honors and monuments.
- None of these traditions explain why the number is sacred.
- They simply state that it always has been.

Forgotten Origin:

- The true origin of the number has been lost.
- Long before the Great Forgetting, the Great Pilgrimage lasted 153 years.
- For generations, the pilgrims repeated the number in prayers, songs, memorials, and ritual observances.
- Then the Great Forgetting came.
- The memory vanished.
- The traditions remained.
- Today, nearly every Kaltheimian recognizes 153 as sacred.
- Almost none remember what it commemorates.

Theological Meaning:

- Over centuries, theologians have associated 153 with faithful endurance, perseverance, remembrance, pilgrimage, and hope carried through suffering.
- Whether these meanings reflect the original intent or arose after the Great Forgetting is impossible to determine.

Campaign Payoff:

- Players may encounter 153 repeatedly before understanding its origin.
- The delayed realization that the Great Pilgrimage lasted exactly 153 years should reveal that the answer has been quietly surrounding them since the beginning.
- This entry embodies the principle: history is gone, tradition remains.

### NAT-011 - Kaltheim-Veloria Relations

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Kaltheim and Veloria are close allies by necessity rather than affection. Their alliance was forged by existential threat, sustained by mutual need, and strained by ideology, technology, espionage, and culture.

Canon Facts:

- Kaltheim and Veloria are allies because they have to be, not because they like one another.

- Once the common enemy was defeated, old distrust immediately resurfaced.
- Kaltheim's initial neutrality during foreign conflicts is consistent with its identity.

- Kaltheim is traditional; Veloria is modern.
- Kaltheim is religious; Veloria is secular.
- Kaltheim emphasizes duty; Veloria emphasizes individual freedom.
- Kaltheim is disciplined; Veloria is informal.
- Kaltheim is cautious; Veloria is innovative.

- Kaltheim refuses to export advanced technology because it believes such technologies are sacred inheritances that must be carefully stewarded.

### GOV-001 - Government of the Sacrament of Kaltheim

## Technology Entries

### TEC-001 - Kaltheimian Technology

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Kaltheimian technology is a mechanical civilization of engineering, metallurgy, chemistry, physics, mechanics, disciplined craftsmanship, and sacredly motivated vocation.

Canon Facts:

- Kaltheim possesses one of the most advanced industrial bases in the known world.
- Its technological achievements are founded almost entirely upon engineering, metallurgy, chemistry, physics, mechanics, and disciplined craftsmanship.
- Though deeply religious, Kaltheimian industry is overwhelmingly mechanical rather than magical.
- The vast majority of Kaltheimian technology is entirely mundane.
- Its foundations include steam engines, diesel engines, electrical power, precision machining, industrial chemistry, heavy metallurgy, large-scale manufacturing, disciplined logistics, armor, engines, and military hardware.
- Common examples include railways, armored vehicles, aircraft, factories, electrical grids, radios, industrial machinery, and precision firearms.
- These are not miracles; they are the products of generations of learning, labor, and disciplined engineering.
- Technology itself is not sacred to Kaltheim.
- The Iron Church teaches that humanity was created with the capacity to build.
- Bridges, engines, cultivated fields, and cities are viewed as expressions of humanity fulfilling the purpose entrusted to it by the First Ones.
- Building well is understood as humanity honoring Creation through discipline, cooperation, perseverance, stewardship, and vocation.
- Canon saying: "The machine is not holy. The hands that build it honor Creation."
- Kaltheim draws a sharp distinction between divine miracles and arcane magic.
- Divine power is understood as a gift granted through the Communion and exercised in faithful service.
- Arcane magic is viewed with profound suspicion by many Kaltheimians.
- Many within the Iron Church believe arcane magic encourages dependence upon powers outside humanity's proper vocation, while engineering and craftsmanship cultivate the virtues of discipline, cooperation, and stewardship.
- This is a dominant theological concern rather than an absolute condemnation; dissenting theologians, reformers, and exceptional arcane practitioners may exist within the setting.
- Canon saying: "A machine built by ten thousand hands honors Creation. A spell cast by one mortal honors only himself."
- Engineers, machinists, blacksmiths, architects, physicians, and industrial workers occupy places of great respect within Kaltheimian society.
- Their work is not considered miraculous; it is considered faithful.
- Engineering is treated as a vocation and a civic service: to build well is to serve well, and to serve well is to honor the Creator.

Consequences:

- Kaltheim excels at heavy industry, metallurgy, engines, armor, military hardware, logistics, and mass production.
- Kaltheim's reluctance to export advanced technology comes from stewardship doctrine as much as distrust.
- Kaltheim's philosophy reinforces the Eismark theme that humanity's greatness comes not from effortless power, but from the patient work of building civilization together.

### TEC-002 - Velorian Technology

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Velorian technology is defined by adaptation, experimentation, commercial innovation, and the practical blending of conventional engineering with widespread arcane practice.

Canon Facts:

- Where Kaltheim builds through centuries of preservation and refinement, Veloria advances through adaptation, experimentation, and innovation.
- Velorian industry combines conventional engineering with widespread arcane practice, creating technologies that are often ingenious, unconventional, and rapidly evolving.
- Velorian engineering is driven less by tradition than by necessity.
- Engineers are encouraged to experiment, modify existing designs, solve problems creatively, and improve upon imported ideas.
- Canon saying: "If it works, improve it."
- Unlike Kaltheim, whose greatest technologies descend directly from Ancient Kaltheim, Veloria developed much of its industrial base independently after the Great Forgetting.
- Where foreign technology is obtained, Velorian engineers rarely copy it exactly; they adapt it to suit Velorian needs.
- Velorian industrial culture is known for flexibility, craftsmanship, rapid iteration, and practical problem solving.
- Veloria places little distinction between engineering and arcane study.
- Magic is viewed as another discipline of knowledge.
- Engineers, artificers, and mages frequently collaborate.
- Arcane techniques are commonly integrated into manufacturing, communications, transportation, medicine, and industrial processes.
- Magic is treated as a practical tool rather than a theological danger.
- Veloria's approach to technology is a major source of dispute with Kaltheim.
- Kaltheim believes many ancient technologies are dangerous inheritances that must be carefully preserved and controlled.
- Veloria argues that knowledge belongs to humanity and that progress cannot flourish if one nation alone decides who is worthy to possess it.
- Many Velorians view Kaltheim's policies as paternalistic rather than malicious.
- Common Velorian complaint: "They act like they're the only adults left in the world."
- Veloria reverse-engineered firearms from smuggled Kaltheimian technology.
- Criminal organizations acquired damaged weapons, industrial machinery, replacement components, technical drawings, and manufacturing techniques.
- Velorian engineers painstakingly reverse-engineered these technologies.
- Rather than reproducing Kaltheim's standardized military equipment, Velorian firearms emphasize craftsmanship, elegance, personalization, ease of modification, and user culture.
- Velorian industry favors skilled workshops, private manufacturers, competitive innovation, independent inventors, and commercial production.
- Products often vary between manufacturers.
- Individual gunsmiths, machinists, and artificers are often known for distinctive styles and improvements.
- This contrasts sharply with Kaltheim's highly standardized industrial doctrine.
- Approximately forty years ago, Veloria enacted the Firearms and Spirits Act, prohibiting civilian ownership of firearms and the production and sale of alcohol.
- The legislation was intended to reduce organized violence and improve public order.
- Instead, organized crime came to dominate the black market, illegal firearms became widespread, prohibition became inconsistently enforced, corruption became commonplace, and public confidence in the law steadily eroded.
- Military and law enforcement remain exempt from the restrictions.
- Velorian culture treats technology as freedom, opportunity, and future-making.
- Where Kaltheim teaches stewardship, Veloria teaches opportunity.
- Many Velorians believe technological progress should remain open to everyone capable of advancing it.
- To Velorians, invention represents liberty, self-improvement, economic prosperity, and individual initiative.
- A machine is valued not because it preserves tradition, but because it makes tomorrow better than today.
- Kaltheim builds machines intended to last centuries; Veloria builds machines intended to be improved next year.
- Common joke: "Kaltheim builds the perfect machine. We build the next one."
- Neither Kaltheim nor Veloria is simply more advanced than the other.
- Kaltheim excels at heavy industry, metallurgy, engines, armor, military hardware, logistics, and mass production.
- Veloria excels at miniaturization, consumer technology, communications, optics, chemical engineering, arcane engineering, and commercial innovation.

Consequences:

- Velorian innovation is fast, flexible, and difficult for the state to fully contain.
- Veloria's technology culture reinforces its identity as the Commonwealth: free, ambitious, experimental, and commercially energetic.
- The technological philosophies of Kaltheim and Veloria mirror their cultures: stewardship against innovation, preservation against progress, standardization against adaptation, mechanical mastery against mechanical-arcane integration, duty against opportunity, and collective achievement against individual ingenuity.
- Each nation remains advanced in different directions, reinforcing why each still has something the other wants.

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: The Armed Forces of the Sacrament are the professional military forces of Kaltheim, organized not for conquest but for the sacred duty of vigilance.

- Their purpose is vigilance, defense, and preservation of humanity rather than conquest.

- Kaltheim expects citizen service and provides military training broadly, but its standing military is composed of career professionals.
- Kaltheimian soldiers are among the most disciplined and extensively trained troops in the world.
- Promotion is based primarily on competence, faith, leadership, experience, and character rather than birth or political influence.
- Doctrine emphasizes the swift and total destruction of existential threats through combined arms warfare, superior logistics, heavy artillery, mechanized offensives, air superiority, divine support, fortress defense, sieges, trench warfare, and logistics-driven attrition.
- Every branch exists to support the others; no arm fights alone.
- Overwhelming firepower means not only concentrated firepower, but also methodical destruction of an enemy's ability to continue fighting.
- When rapid victory cannot be achieved, Kaltheim readily transitions to prolonged siege and entrenchment warfare, relying on superior engineering, logistics, and industrial capacity to exhaust the enemy.

- Paladins are integrated throughout the chain of command.

- The Iron Fleet is Kaltheim's naval branch, responsible for surface fleets, arctic operations, coastal defense, naval aviation, and maritime logistics.
- The Skyward Host is Kaltheim's aerial branch, responsible for fighters, bombers, airships, reconnaissance, strategic transport, and air superiority.

- Kaltheim does not glorify conquest; it glorifies faithful service, and war is one of the highest forms that service can take when humanity is threatened.
- This contrasts with the Blood, whose violence is purposeless destruction, while Kaltheim's ideal is disciplined, sacrificial protection.

### SOC-002 - Kaltheim-Veloria Technology Dispute

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: The Kaltheim-Veloria technology dispute is a major ideological and political conflict over whether advanced engineering, metallurgy, firearms, and sacred industrial techniques should be guarded as dangerous inheritances or shared as common human progress.

Canon Facts:

- Kaltheim possesses engineering knowledge, metallurgy, and sacred industrial techniques that no other nation can fully replicate.
- Kaltheim does not export its most advanced technologies.
- Kaltheim's refusal is not primarily greed; Kaltheimians see themselves as stewards rather than owners.
- Many Kaltheimian technologies are regarded as dangerous inheritances from Ancient Kaltheim, preserved at tremendous cost since the Great Pilgrimage.
- Kaltheim believes unrestricted dissemination risks another civilizational catastrophe.
- Kaltheim sees itself as humanity's shield and heir to Ancient Kaltheim.
- A common Kaltheimian attitude is: "These machines survived because we protected them. We will not allow the world to misuse them."
- Veloria believes technology belongs to humanity, innovation should be shared, knowledge should not be hoarded, and progress is impossible if one nation decides who is worthy.
- Many Velorians view Kaltheim as arrogant and paternalistic rather than evil.
- A common Velorian complaint is: "They act like they're the only adults left in the world."

Smuggling Trade:

- Kaltheim exports only limited quantities of raw materials such as steel, specialized alloys, and certain machine tools.
- Kaltheim does not export military technology, sacred construction techniques, or advanced weapon designs.
- Criminal organizations smuggle firearms, blueprints, machine tools, industrial components, replacement parts, and occasionally damaged Kaltheim weapons.
- The Iron Church treats this smuggling as theft of sacred property.
- Veloria officially condemns the smuggling trade.
- Unofficially, Velorian authorities often do not investigate very hard.

Birth of Velorian Firearms:

- Veloria did not invent firearms independently.
- Veloria reverse-engineered them from stolen receivers, copied mechanisms, salvaged barrels, recovered blueprints, and experimental reproductions.
- This origin explains why Kaltheim's firearms appear standardized and industrial while Veloria's are more artisanal and individually named.
- Kaltheim invented the rifle.
- Veloria perfected making it beautiful.

Connection to Prohibition:

- Kaltheim developed firearms.
- Smugglers stole examples.
- Velorian engineers copied them.
- Crime syndicates armed themselves first.
- The Velorian government panicked and outlawed firearms.
- The prohibition produced the opposite effect: criminals retained access while lawful citizens were disarmed.
- Alcohol prohibition emerged from similar reformist logic, with reformers arguing that alcohol fueled organized crime while firearms fueled political violence.
- Instead, organized crime grew richer and public frustration increased.

Ideological Balance:

- Kaltheim is not simply selfish; it has spent centuries preserving dangerous knowledge and genuinely fears misuse.
- Veloria is not simply reckless; it objects to one nation claiming authority over what humanity may learn.
- Both positions are understandable, making the dispute an ideological conflict rather than a simple rivalry.

### LAW-001 - The Public Safety and Temperance Act

Canon Status: Locked

Common Name: The Firearms and Spirits Act

Summary: The Public Safety and Temperance Act is one of the most controversial laws in modern Veloria. Enacted approximately forty years before the campaign, it prohibits civilian firearm ownership, civilian firearm manufacture, and the production and sale of alcoholic beverages. Though still law, the Act is now widely ignored, selectively enforced, and viewed by much of the public as a failure.

Canon Facts:

- The Act was enacted approximately forty years before the campaign.
- Its official name is the Public Safety and Temperance Act.
- Its common public nickname is the Firearms and Spirits Act.
- It prohibits civilian ownership of firearms.
- It prohibits civilian manufacture of firearms.
- It prohibits the production, sale, and commercial distribution of alcoholic beverages.
- Military personnel and law enforcement agencies are exempt from the Act.
- The law remains officially in force.

Intended Purpose:

- The Velorian government justified the Act as necessary to promote public safety, social stability, and the reduction of organized violence.

Consequences:

- The Act produced the opposite effect in many areas.
- Organized crime expanded dramatically.
- Firearms smuggling became one of the republic's most profitable black-market industries.
- Illegal distilleries and speakeasies became commonplace.
- Corruption spread throughout local and national government.
- Public confidence in the government steadily declined.
- Although officially prohibited, firearms are readily available through criminal organizations and illicit markets.

Modern Reality:

- Officially, civilian firearm ownership is illegal.
- Officially, alcohol remains prohibited.
- Unofficially, many citizens possess illegal firearms.
- Criminal syndicates openly traffic weapons.
- Entire neighborhoods ignore prohibition.
- Speakeasies and illegal taverns operate throughout Veloria.
- Enforcement varies dramatically depending on location and political influence.
- Common illegal firearms include rotators, slugthrowers, barkers, and longstrikes.

Government Corruption:

- Many public officials continue to publicly support the Act while privately violating it.
- Examples include accepting bribes from smuggling organizations, possessing illegal firearms, protecting criminal enterprises, facilitating weapons trafficking, and quietly purchasing military equipment through corrupt channels.
- This hypocrisy has become one of the defining political scandals of the republic.

Public Opinion:

- Most Velorians believe the Act has failed.
- A common saying is: "The law only disarmed the people who followed it."
- Another common saying is: "If the government won't protect us, we'll protect ourselves."
- As strange and increasingly dangerous phenomena begin appearing across Eismark, public support for repealing the Act continues to grow.

International Perspective:

- Kaltheim views the Act as evidence of Veloria's political weakness.
- Among Kaltheim's leadership, it is commonly argued that a nation unable to enforce its own laws cannot be trusted with advanced military technologies.
- This perception further reinforces Kaltheim's longstanding refusal to export its most advanced weapons, engineering, and sacred technologies to Veloria.

### LAW-002 - Kaltheimian Justice System

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: Justice in the Sacrament is founded on the belief that law derives from doctrine.

Canon Facts:

- There is no true separation between religion and law in Kaltheim.
- Ordinary crimes are tried before Ecclesiastical Magistrates: ordained Iron Church members trained in theology, jurisprudence, and civil law.
- Civic courts handle theft, assault, property disputes, contracts, family law, and inheritance.
- Common saying: "A just society requires righteous judges."
- Military tribunals handle desertion, insubordination, dereliction of duty, conduct unbecoming, and wartime offenses by members of the armed forces.
- Military tribunals are usually overseen by senior officers, military magistrates, and Templar representatives.
- Ecclesiastical Courts are the highest courts of the Sacrament and adjudicate heresy, sedition, corruption, crimes against the Church, major constitutional disputes, and doctrine.
- Cases involving existential threats, relics, Blood corruption, or Veins almost always fall under Ecclesiastical jurisdiction.
- The Inquisition investigates but does not normally judge cases.
- Kaltheimian law emphasizes Truth, Repentance, Restitution, and Protection of the Sacrament.
- Punishment is meant to restore the individual and the community when possible.
- Crimes threatening humanity's survival are punished with extreme severity.
- Common saying: "Mercy is a virtue. Vigilance is a necessity."
- Capital punishment is uncommon but reserved for irredeemable betrayals of humanity, the Sacrament, or the Vigil.
- Capital crimes may include treason, murder, foreign espionage, deliberate heresy, severe official or clerical corruption, wartime desertion, collaboration with existential threats, smuggling forbidden relics or Blood-tainted artifacts, knowingly concealing Blood corruption, and acts endangering the Communion or Sacrament.
- Kaltheim distinguishes error from heresy: error is corrected through instruction, penance, and repentance; heresy is deliberate teaching of false doctrine judged harmful to the Sacrament.
- Common saying: "Steel may kill a man. Falsehood may kill a people."
- Penal practice favors repentance, reintegration, imprisonment, labor service, military penal service, public penance, and restitution until an offender is judged unrepentant, corrupt, or existentially dangerous.

Consequences:

- Kaltheim is not cruel for cruelty's sake, but it is uncompromising when survival is at stake.
- From a Velorian perspective the system often appears authoritarian; from a Kaltheimian perspective it is the necessary architecture of civilization's survival.

### CAM-002 - The Bar

Canon Status: Locked

Summary: The Bar is a sanctuary for the forgotten, manifested through a surviving fragment of Uriel's fractured consciousness.

Canon Facts:

- The Bar is not a fixed place.
- It has no permanent location, appearance, or name.
- Everyone who has found it eventually refers to it simply as The Bar.
- The Bar always manifests in a form appropriate to its surroundings.
- Possible appearances include a warm jazz speakeasy in Veloria, a stone mountain tavern in Kaltheim, a lonely roadside inn upon the frontier, a weathered harbor pub, or an isolated wilderness lodge.
- The architecture changes, but the feeling never does.
- Every visitor remembers seeing a sign above the entrance, but no two visitors remember the same name.
- Recalled names may include The Rusty Nail, The Wayfarer's Rest, or The Hearth.
- Those who have visited The Bar never mistake it for another building.
- Recognition is immediate and requires no explanation.
- The Bartender is always present when The Bar appears.
- The Bartender may wear different clothing and stand within different surroundings, but he is always quietly cleaning a glass, welcoming weary travelers, remembering people he should not remember, and never remembering himself.
- The Bar is always warm, safe, welcoming, and strangely familiar.
- Music, food, furniture, and architecture may differ from manifestation to manifestation.
- The Bar cannot be intentionally revisited.
- Once a visitor leaves, The Bar disappears.
- Returning moments later reveals only an ordinary road, empty field, abandoned building, or untouched wilderness.
- No known method exists to locate The Bar by choice.
- The Bar is not unique to the player characters.
- Scattered historical accounts describe impossible taverns appearing during moments of great hardship.
- Most people dismiss these accounts as coincidence.
- Rules include no violence, speak truthfully, respect the dead, and everyone eventually leaves.
- Living and dead may briefly meet there.
- Visitors have a usual drink, and the Bartender knows it.

Why The Bar Appears:

- No one knows why The Bar appears when it does.
- Many believe it comes to those who are lost, not merely lost upon the road, but lost within themselves.
- Whether this is the will of Uriel, the instinct of one of his surviving fragments, or something else entirely remains unknown.
- The Bar should not be treated as a place consciously dispatched by the Communion.
- The Bar never appears when the players need shelter. It appears when they need to remember who they are.
- The Bar remembers what the world has forgotten.

The Bar Remembers:

- The Bar is not static.
- As the player characters recover lost memories and draw nearer to the truth, The Bar subtly changes.
- Early in the campaign, The Bar feels almost impossibly quiet, nearly empty, and almost untouched.
- As the campaign progresses, more patrons appear, music returns, laughter fills the room, photographs decorate the walls, forgotten keepsakes appear upon shelves, and old stories are told once more.
- Eventually the players may notice that many of these objects belong to them.
- Some objects depict moments they cannot remember.
- Others show companions they have never met, or so they believe.
- The Bar evolves with memory and hope.
- Early campaign manifestations may feel empty, cold, sparsely lit, and nearly patronless.
- Later manifestations may include music, conversation, a warm fireplace, laughter, children, old veterans, families reunited, and the Bartender actually smiling.
- The Bar is not merely remembering; Uriel is healing.

GM Guidance:

- The Bar should never become routine.
- Its appearances should remain rare.
- Ideally, The Bar appears after major revelations, during moments of profound uncertainty, following great sacrifice, or when the player characters are emotionally lost.
- Its rarity preserves its mystery.
