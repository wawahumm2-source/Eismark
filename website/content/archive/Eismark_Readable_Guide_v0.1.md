# Eismark Reader Guide v0.1

This is the reader-facing companion to the Eismark Canon Database. It presents the same setting information in a campaign-sourcebook format for easier reading, browsing, and table use.

The ECD Archive remains the source-of-truth database. This guide is updated in parallel when reader-facing canon changes. If the two ever disagree, the ECD Archive takes precedence until this guide is corrected.

## Using This Guide

- Entry IDs are kept in headings so each section can be cross-referenced with the ECD Archive.
- Most source IDs, audit wiring, and administrative fields are removed from the main text.
- Entries marked Under Review are useful, but not fully locked.
- Entries marked Archived Non-Canon are preserved development history, not active setting truth.

## Status Key

- Locked: current canon or finalized supporting record.
- Under Review: not final yet; usually pending map, numbering, or equipment work.
- DEV: active design work, not canon yet.
- Archived Non-Canon: preserved development history, not active canon.

## History

Eismark is a world built on broken memory. Its history begins in divine creation, passes through angelic sacrifice, and arrives in an age of rifles, railways, nations, and unanswered questions.

### HIS-001 — Creation

The Creator brings Creation into existence, beginning reality and the Age of Creation.

**Lore**

- The Creator creates Creation.
- Reality begins.
- The Age of Creation begins.

**Consequences**

- The First Ones can now be created by the Creator and sent into Vardheim to shape it.

**See Also:** COS-001 — Creation; COS-002 — The First Ones; HIS-002 — The First Ones Are Created

### HIS-002 — The First Ones Are Created

The First Ones are the highest order of angels, created directly by the Creator and entrusted with shaping Creation.

**Lore**

- The First Ones are angels.
- The First Ones are also known as Archangels or High Angels.
- They are the highest order of angels.
- They are created directly by the Creator.
- After Creation exists, the Creator sends the First Ones into Vardheim to shape it.
- They are entrusted with shaping Creation.
- The First Ones shape Creation and guide mortal civilization.
- The First Ones create the lesser angels.

**Consequences**

- The First Ones become the divine founders of early Creation and the highest class within the angelic hierarchy.

**See Also:** COS-002 — The First Ones; REL-001 — The First Ones Pantheon; CAP-002 — Angelic Taxonomy; CAP-003 — Origin of the First Ones

### HIS-003 — Lesser Angels Are Created

The First Ones create hundreds of lesser angels to aid in shaping Creation.

**Lore**

- Lesser angels assist the First Ones.
- Their creation accelerates the shaping of Creation.
- Many later angelic vessels and dormant angels are relevant to the Communion and Joivians.

**Consequences**

- Lesser angels become central to the Long Vigil, the Communion, and Joivian construction.

**See Also:** COS-003 — Lesser Angels; HIS-004 — The World Is Shaped; HIS-009 — The Sacrifice of the Angels; MIL-006 — Joivians

### HIS-004 — The World Is Shaped

The First Ones and lesser angels shape the physical world and establish its natural order.

**Lore**

- Lands, seas, mountains, and natural law are established.
- Creation becomes ready for mortal life.

**Consequences**

- Mortal civilization becomes possible.

**See Also:** HIS-005 — Mortal Peoples Are Created; HIS-006 — The First Age Begins; COS-001 — Creation

### HIS-006 — The First Age Begins

Mortal civilizations flourish beneath angelic guidance during a mythic age of learning and prosperity. The First Ones walk openly among mortals, and knowledge, engineering, philosophy, medicine, art, agriculture, and record keeping all flourish.

Ancient Kaltheim exists as a great center of learning before becoming a fortress society. Mortal pursuit of universal truth eventually causes Creation to become visible to the Entity.

### HIS-007 — The Veins Open

Fractures in reality called Veins open, allowing the Blood to enter Creation.

**Lore**

- The Entity becomes aware of Creation through the actions of mortal scholars.
- The scholars do not summon the Entity; their discoveries cause Creation to become visible to it.
- The Veins open between Creation and the Entity.
- The Blood enters the world through the Veins.

**Consequences**

- The Long Vigil begins.
- Reality, landscapes, bodies, minds, souls, and memories become vulnerable to corruption.

**See Also:** COS-004 — The Entity; COS-005 — The Blood; MON-001 — Bloodspawn; CAP-004 — Opening of the Veins

### HIS-008 — The Long Vigil Begins

The First Ones, lesser angels, and mortals wage war against the open Veins and the Blood.

**Lore**

- The Blood cannot be permanently stopped through force.
- The struggle lasts ages or centuries depending on source wording.
- The conflict devastates civilization.

**Consequences**

- The angels eventually sacrifice themselves to seal reality.

**See Also:** HIS-009 — The Sacrifice of the Angels; COS-005 — The Blood; MIL-006 — Joivians

### HIS-009 — The Sacrifice of the Angels

The First Ones sacrifice themselves, forming the Communion and sealing reality.

**Lore**

- The Veins cannot be closed by force.
- The First Ones sacrifice nearly all divine power.
- The First Ones pour themselves into Vardheim.
- The Communion is formed by this sacrifice.
- Reality is sealed through the Communion.
- Many become white stone statues or enter deep dormancy.
- The Communion seals the Veins and prevents the Entity from fully entering Creation.

**Consequences**

- The Blood already inside Creation remains.
- The Lingering War follows.

**See Also:** COS-006 — The Communion; HIS-010 — The Lingering War Begins; NPC-001 — Uriel, the Rememberer; CAP-005 — Communion

### HIS-010 — The Lingering War Begins

Though the Veins are sealed, Blood already present in the world remains and continues to ravage mortal civilization.

**Lore**

- Most mortal civilization is sterilized or destroyed.
- Ancient Kaltheim becomes humanity's last great beacon or bastion.
- Kaltheim preserves angelic knowledge and assumes the burden of vigilance.

**Consequences**

- Ancient Kaltheim begins seeking a final solution to the Blood.

**See Also:** HIS-011 — The Joivian Project; MIL-006 — Joivians; GEO-004 — Ancient Kaltheim

### HIS-011 — The Joivian Project

Ancient Kaltheim creates the Joivians as humanity's final hope against the Blood.

**Lore**

- Joivians are holy war machines.
- Joivians are built around dormant vessels of lesser angels.
- Uriel is the sole exception.
- Uriel's Joivian alone is constructed around the vessel of a First One.
- This makes Uriel's Joivian unique.
- Official spelling is `Joivian`; `Jovian` is deprecated.

**Consequences**

- Joivians become humanity's greatest weapons against the Blood.
- Joivian construction and spelling are locked by CAP-006 and CAP-007.

**See Also:** MIL-006 — Joivians; NPC-002 — The Abandoned One; CAP-006 — Joivian Construction; CAP-007 — Joivian March

### HIS-012 — Uriel Fractures

During the Lingering War, Uriel began attempting to commune with the Entity and was broken by the contact.

**Lore**

- Uriel is the First One of memory, history, preservation, language, and truth.
- During the Lingering War, Uriel began attempting to commune with the Entity.
- No surviving record explains why he began, what he hoped to learn, how long the communion lasted, or what transpired between them.
- The only universally accepted truth is that Uriel changed.
- Uriel did not become evil.
- Uriel did not betray Creation.
- Uriel broke.
- Whether the communion revealed a terrible truth, exposed him to something no mind could endure, or merely brought two fundamentally incompatible realities into contact remains unknown.
- Even Uriel himself can no longer remember.

**Consequences**

- Uriel's fracture leads into the Great Forgetting.
- Uriel persists in several manifestations.

**See Also:** NPC-001 — Uriel, the Rememberer; HIS-013 — The Joivians March Into the Great Maw; CAM-001 — Vein of Eismark Memory Premise

### HIS-013 — The Joivians March Into the Great Maw

The Joivians march into the Great Maw to confront the remaining Blood.

**Lore**

- Nearly every Joivian marches into the Great Maw and is never seen again.
- The Blood is finally destroyed, contained, or driven back afterward.
- The Joivian containing Uriel remains behind and later becomes known as The Abandoned One.

**Consequences**

- The Lingering War ends.
- The disappearance of the Joivians leads to the Great Pilgrimage.

**See Also:** HIS-014 — The Great Pilgrimage; NPC-002 — The Abandoned One; REL-007 — The Number 153; CAP-008 — Great Forgetting

### HIS-014 — The Great Pilgrimage

The majority of Ancient Kaltheim journeys north in search of the vanished Joivians.

**Lore**

- The Great Pilgrimage begins after the Joivians disappear.
- The pilgrimage lasts 153 years.
- The pilgrims found the modern Sacrament of Kaltheim in the northern mountains.

**Consequences**

- Ancient Kaltheim is largely abandoned.
- The modern Sacrament of Kaltheim emerges as a successor civilization.

**See Also:** NAT-001 — The Sacrament of Kaltheim; GEO-001 — Vardheim and Eismark; CAP-010 — Ancient Kaltheim Geography

### HIS-015 — The Rise of Veloria

Those who remain behind in the ruins of Ancient Kaltheim rebuild over generations, eventually giving rise to Veloria.

**Lore**

- Veloria develops from communities that remain in or rebuild Ancient Kaltheim's ruins.
- Veloria becomes a young republic built on liberty, commerce, and self-determination.
- Veloria measures time through `FC`, the Founding Compact, reflecting civil government rather than religious history.
- The Founding Compact still physically exists.
- Lawyers cite it, politicians swear oaths upon it, and citizens quote it.
- Parts of the Founding Compact no longer make sense to modern Velorians.
- The phrase "May this Compact preserve the memory of our forefathers and the northern homeland from which we came" is commonly interpreted as poetic language.
- The phrase actually refers to Ancient Kaltheim, though modern Velorians no longer realize this.

**Consequences**

- Kaltheim and Veloria become successor civilizations with shared ancient roots and later strained alliance.
- Veloria preserves fragments of its origin through civil documents and legal traditions even when their original context has been forgotten.

**See Also:** NAT-002 — The Commonwealth of Veloria; GEO-006 — Veloria; HIS-016 — The Great Forgetting

### HIS-016 — The Great Forgetting

The world loses much of its collective memory following Uriel's fracture and departure.

The Great Forgetting did not erase civilization. It erased understanding.

**Lore**

- Uriel's departure causes the Great Forgetting.
- The Great Forgetting begins immediately as a metaphysical phenomenon.
- Over generations, it becomes a civilizational collapse.
- Historians later refer to the entire era as The Great Forgetting.
- Knowledge of the First and Second Ages is largely lost.
- Many events pass into myth.
- Languages survived.
- Traditions survived.
- Calendars survived.
- Religions survived.
- Documents survived.
- What was lost was the ability to connect them into a coherent history.

The people of the Third Age inherited a world full of answers without remembering the questions.

**Cultural Memory**

The Great Forgetting did not leave the world empty. It left the world full of fragments.

People still keep holidays whose origins they cannot explain. They still swear old oaths, preserve old place-names, repeat old prayers, use old calendars, and quote documents written by ancestors they no longer truly remember.

This is why modern Kaltheimians can say `1945 AV` without being able to answer a simple question: what was the Vigil?

Different communities give different answers.

- The Iron Church says the Vigil was Michael's eternal watch over mankind.
- Historians call it a prolonged period of conflict in antiquity.
- Ordinary citizens say it was the time their ancestors protected civilization.
- Scholars often admit that they are not entirely sure.

These answers are not contradictions. They are inherited fragments.

Kaltheim measures time through religious history. Veloria measures time through civil government.

Veloria's calendar uses `FC`, the Founding Compact. The contrast is revealing. Kaltheim preserved a sacred memory whose meaning decayed into theology and tradition. Veloria preserved a civil document whose meaning decayed into law and patriotic ritual.

The Founding Compact still physically exists. Lawyers cite it. Politicians swear oaths upon it. Citizens quote it. Yet parts of it no longer make sense.

One passage reads: "May this Compact preserve the memory of our forefathers and the northern homeland from which we came."

Modern Velorians interpret the northern homeland as poetic language. They do not realize it refers literally to Ancient Kaltheim.

Neither Kaltheim nor Veloria fails to understand its traditions. They understand the traditions very well.

They no longer understand why they inherited them.

Even the First Ones possess incomplete memory, because Uriel's fracture wounded the Communion itself.

**Consequences**

- The Third Age begins with fragmented history.
- The campaign's memory mechanics echo this setting-wide wound.
- Ancient truths may survive as dates, prayers, legal formulas, oaths, holidays, ceremonial language, and place-names after their original meanings have been lost.

**Campaign Reveal**

If Uriel ever reads the Founding Compact, he may understand what no modern Velorian does.

"They remembered."

Then, after a pause:

"Longer than I thought."

New civilizations arise upon the ruins of older ages. Kaltheim, Veloria, Gn'ure, and the Sylvan Veil consolidate; dragonborn are displaced from the Shattered Isles; organized religion and kingdoms become defining institutions.

### HIS-018 — Calendrical Systems

After the Great Forgetting, different civilizations continued measuring years but redefined Year One around the event they believed mattered most. Kaltheim uses `AV`, After Vigil. Veloria uses `FC`, the Founding Compact.

The calendars share ancient roots, but each culture interprets its inherited fragments differently. Kaltheim anchors time to faith, endurance, and guardianship of Creation. Veloria anchors time to law, liberty, and the republic.

Kaltheim's current atmosphere draws from a cultural 1938-1945: militarized, ancient, sacrificial, fortified, and standing near the edge of catastrophic war. Veloria's current atmosphere draws from a cultural 1928: art deco skylines, jazz, electric lights, department stores, subways, stock exchanges, modern industry, and a future that still seems bright. These are aesthetic inspirations, not literal Earth equivalencies.

### HIS-019 — The Industrial Age

Industry, firearms, railroads, airships, mechanized logistics, and modern state capacity transform Eismark before the Great Continental War. Heavy industry, precision manufacturing, modern metallurgy, rail systems, communications, and military logistics spread across the continent.

Kaltheim deepens its tradition of disciplined industrial engineering. Veloria expands commercial innovation, communications, consumer technology, and arcane engineering. Voss, Rauk, Lan Cao, and Ostmark develop the capacities that later support the Imperial League.

**See Also:** TEC-001 — Kaltheimian Technology; TEC-002 — Velorian Technology; NAT-001 — The Sacrament of Kaltheim; NAT-002 — The Commonwealth of Veloria; MIL-007 — Automatons

### HIS-020 — The Great Continental War

The Imperial League rises, wages continental war, and falls after defeat by the Grand Alliance of Kaltheim and Veloria. The League consists of Voss, Rauk, Lan Cao, and Ostmark. It expands aggressively, deploys Automatons and Automaton Colossi, and forces Kaltheim and Veloria into an uneasy alliance.

The League's defeat leads to the Treaty of Karhold, the collapse of the League into successor states, and the Great Silence. Kaltheim and Veloria remain allies by necessity afterward, though old distrust returns almost immediately.

The Treaty of Karhold prohibits the creation of new autonomous military constructs after the Great Continental War. It bans military Automaton production and becomes one of the defining postwar legal structures of the modern era.

The ban creates fertile ground for black projects, espionage, secret factories, and rogue states.

## Cosmology

The cosmology of Eismark is not distant theology. Creation, the Entity, the Blood, and the Communion all shape the lives of ordinary people.

### COS-001 — Creation

Creation is the ordered reality brought into being by the Creator.

**Lore**

- Creation has natural laws and boundaries.
- The Blood is incompatible with Creation.
- The Entity exists outside Creation.

**See Also:** COS-004 — The Entity; COS-005 — The Blood

### COS-002 — The First Ones

The First Ones are the highest order of angels within Eismark's cosmology.

**Lore**

- Angel is a broad term encompassing all divine celestial beings created by the Creator.
- Angels are divided into two primary classes: First Ones and Lesser Angels.
- The First Ones are also known as Archangels or High Angels.
- The First Ones are the highest order of angels.
- The First Ones were created directly by the Creator.
- The First Ones were entrusted with shaping Creation, guiding early Creation, creating the lesser angels, waging the First War, and founding the Communion.
- References to "Angels walked Vardheim" may refer broadly to both First Ones and Lesser Angels.

**See Also:** REL-001 — The First Ones Pantheon; COS-003 — Lesser Angels; CAP-002 — Angelic Taxonomy; CAP-003 — Origin of the First Ones

### COS-003 — Lesser Angels

Lesser angels are created by the First Ones to assist in shaping Creation.

**Lore**

- They are numerous.
- They are divine servants and caretakers of Creation.
- They are subordinate to the First Ones.
- They serve as emissaries, guardians, soldiers, and attendants.
- Many become dormant during the Sacrifice.
- Some angelic vessels are used in Joivians.

**See Also:** HIS-003 — Lesser Angels Are Created; HIS-009 — The Sacrifice of the Angels; MIL-006 — Joivians

### COS-004 — The Entity

The Entity is an unknowable outside reality whose presence is catastrophic to Creation.

**Working Canon**

- The Entity is not a god, demon, or force of evil.
- It is not malicious.
- It does not hate Creation.
- It possesses no recognizable hatred, mercy, ambition, or vengeance.
- It has no recognizable human motivations.
- It exists beyond the laws that govern Creation.
- The Entity and Creation are fundamentally incompatible.
- It became aware of Creation through the actions of mortal scholars.
- Mortal scholars did not summon it; their actions made Creation visible to it.
- What mortals perceive as curiosity may simply be the way the Entity exists.
- Its attention should not be mistaken for intent.
- Wherever the Entity comes into contact with Creation, reality begins to fail.
- Corruption is an inevitable consequence of contact.
- It remains fundamentally unknowable.

The Entity is not trying to destroy Creation. Creation simply cannot survive its presence.

**See Also:** COS-005 — The Blood; MON-001 — Bloodspawn; MON-002 — Aberrations; CAM-001 — Vein of Eismark Memory Premise

### COS-005 — The Blood

The Blood is the physical manifestation of the Entity's corruption within Creation, an impossible substance that seeps from the wounded Veins of the world.

**Lore**

- It has no will, intelligence, or goals.
- It is not a natural substance.
- It is incompatible with Creation and exists outside the laws of reality.
- It is not always liquid and may flow when it should not or remain still when it should move.
- It displays impossible shifting colors that the mortal mind struggles to comprehend.
- It is not simply poisonous or magical; it is a force of corruption that rewrites reality itself.
- It corrupts flesh, mind, memory, soul, landscape, and reality.
- Rather than infecting victims, it rewrites them into something they were never meant to become.
- It causes sensory pain and an overwhelming feeling of wrongness.
- Its many hues are not separate substances, but different manifestations of the same cosmic corruption.
- Known manifestations are described poetically as bleeding without wounding, shining without light, moving without moving, whispering without voice, existing without reason, and not being meant to be seen.
- Exposure may leave victims partially aware, trapped inside changing forms, or erased entirely.
- Concentrated Blood can cause headaches, nausea, nosebleeds, dizziness, disturbing dreams, and sensory pain simply from looking at it.
- Corruption often begins subtly, with the mind deteriorating before the body.
- Early signs often include obsessive thoughts, intrusive dreams, personality shifts, dissociation, and emotional instability.
- Transformation is continuous and unstable.
- Blood corruption progresses through four observed stages: Whispering, Alteration, Fracture, and Blooming.
- Whispering causes dreams, compulsions, and intrusive thoughts.
- Alteration begins physical corruption as flesh changes, fingers lengthen, posture shifts, skin discolors, senses distort, and the mind starts to unravel.
- Fracture brings pain, obsession, violence, loss of stable reality perception, and rapid changes to mind and body.
- Blooming is complete corruption, where the former self ceases to be recognizable and a Bloodspawn is born.
- When the Blood touches land, affected regions may exhibit unnatural silence, humming skies, fleeing wildlife, dying or twisted vegetation, distorted space and time, and a profound sense that reality has become fundamentally wrong.
- Common witness phrases include "The woods were silent," "The ground was screaming," "The sky was humming," and "The hills were looking at us."
- Blood-tainted landscapes feel like reality rejecting an infection it was never meant to contain.
- Nothing that enters Blood-touched places leaves unchanged.
- The Blood is a cosmic force of unmaking that erases and overwrites Creation with something alien and impossible.
- The Blood does not create monsters in the ordinary sense; it creates failed attempts at something new.
- References to the Blood carrying a will to unmake Creation are poetic descriptions of the Entity's corrupting influence, not evidence that the Blood itself has independent agency.
- The Veins ceased producing new Blood after reality was sealed.
- Blood already present within Creation remained trapped there.
- Public knowledge is fragmentary: most people know only that a great darkness, plague, divine punishment, demon-war, or terrible age of war threatened civilization long ago.
- Most ordinary people have never heard the term "the Blood"; those who have usually assume it is metaphor, superstition, or allegory.
- The Iron Church, Uriel's Testament, the Inquisition of the Vigil, and the Dreamers of Azhul know more, but each preserves incomplete or distorted fragments.

**See Also:** MON-001 — Bloodspawn; GEO-012 — The Ashlands; CAP-011 — Blood Lifecycle; CAP-012 — Bloodspawn

### COS-006 — The Communion

The Communion is the metaphysical mechanism formed by the First Ones' sacrifice to seal reality.

**Lore**

- It is created when the First Ones pour themselves into Vardheim.
- It is not a separate miracle from the First Ones' sacrifice; it is the mechanism of that sacrifice.
- The sequence is: First Ones sacrifice, Communion formed, reality sealed.
- Uriel becomes part of it.
- The Bar exists primarily within it.
- It prevents the Entity from fully entering Creation.

**See Also:** HIS-009 — The Sacrifice of the Angels; NPC-001 — Uriel, the Rememberer; CAM-002 — The Bar; CAP-005 — Communion

## Religion

Religion in Eismark is practical, political, and deeply woven into daily life. Faith builds nations, consecrates steel, and remembers the dead.

**Lore**

- Luke: methodology, science, engineering, logic, ordered thought.

### REL-006 — Uriel, Angel of Memory

Uriel is revered throughout Creation as the Angel of Memory, the Eternal Witness, the Archivist, and the Keeper of Histories. Before the Great Forgetting, he was the living archive of Creation, preserving the memories, histories, triumphs, failures, and identities of all peoples.

**Lore**

- Uriel is one of the First Ones.
- His sacred duty was not to rule, but to remember.
- His symbol is an illuminated manuscript.
- He is patron of memory, history, tradition, learning, scholarship, record keeping, genealogy, and preservation.
- He is especially revered by historians, archivists, librarians, scholars, scribes, teachers, students, and genealogists.
- Common prayers include: "Help me remember," "Preserve what must not be forgotten," and "May the wisdom of those who came before guide me."
- During the Communion, Uriel willingly sacrificed a portion of his divine being to help seal Creation against the Entity.
- As ages passed, his divine consciousness became inseparable from the world's memory.
- During the Lingering War, Uriel began attempting to commune with the Entity.
- No surviving record explains why he began, what he hoped to learn, how long the communion lasted, or what transpired between them.
- Uriel did not become evil and did not betray Creation; he broke.
- Whether his communion revealed some terrible truth, exposed him to something no mind could endure, or merely brought two fundamentally incompatible realities into contact remains unknown.
- Even Uriel himself can no longer remember.
- When Uriel separated himself from the Communion, memory itself broke.
- Entire civilizations disappeared from history, libraries became meaningless, genealogies dissolved, kingdoms forgot their founders, and even angels found their memories unraveling.
- The Great Forgetting unfolded over generations and became the defining event that ended the old world and ushered in the Third Age.
- The Great Pilgrimage began after Uriel's disappearance, as the people of Ancient Kaltheim searched for the vanished Joivian Hosts, the missing Uriel, and remnants of the old world.
- The Great Pilgrimage lasted one hundred fifty-three years.
- The Bartender is a surviving fragment of Uriel's shattered consciousness.
- The Bar is widely believed to exist within the remnants of Uriel's shattered mind.
- Uriel still exists somewhere, fractured, isolated, and profoundly damaged.
- He exists across memory, history, dreams, and reality, and time no longer binds him completely.
- He may remember future events, forget the distant past, speak to the dead, mistake strangers for lifelong friends, or recall people who no longer exist.
- Uriel embodies memory, loss, identity, nostalgia, regret, loneliness, preservation, and hope despite sorrow.

**Sourcebook Note**

Uriel's tragedy is not that he chose to abandon Creation. His tragedy is that he broke. When the Angel of Memory broke, memory itself broke with him.

### REL-007 — The Number 153

Among the faithful of the Iron Church, 153 is regarded as one of the holiest numbers in Kaltheimian tradition.

It appears everywhere: monastery architecture, funerary rites, military ceremonies, pilgrimage traditions, illuminated manuscripts, rosaries, sacred measurements, and liturgical calendars.

Its use is so widespread that few people ever question it.

**Common Traditions**

- Monastic prayer cycles traditionally consist of 153 invocations.
- Pilgrims ascend 153 ceremonial steps before entering many great shrines.
- Memorial services often feature 153 candles.
- Bells ring 153 times during the highest days of remembrance.
- Consecrated rosaries traditionally contain 153 beads.
- Military memorials frequently incorporate the number into ceremonial honors and monuments.

None of these traditions explain why the number is sacred.

They simply state that it always has been.

**The Forgotten Origin**

The true origin of the number has been lost.

Long before the Great Forgetting, the Great Pilgrimage lasted one hundred fifty-three years. For generations, the pilgrims repeated the number in prayers, songs, memorials, and ritual observances.

Then the Great Forgetting came.

The memory vanished.

The traditions remained.

Today, nearly every Kaltheimian recognizes 153 as a sacred number. Almost none remember what it commemorates.

**Theological Meaning**

Over centuries, theologians have come to associate 153 with faithful endurance, perseverance, remembrance, pilgrimage, and hope carried through suffering.

Whether these meanings reflect the original intent or arose after the Great Forgetting is impossible to determine.

**Campaign Payoff**

One day, a priest may quietly count the 153 beads of a rosary before offering a prayer.

Uriel watches in silence.

After a long pause, he asks, "Do they still remember?"

The priest looks up. "Remember what?"

Uriel smiles sadly.

"Nothing."

And the prayer continues.

History is gone. Tradition remains.

### REL-008 — Worship in the Iron Church

Iron Church worship centers on the Divine Liturgy: structured ritual, icons, incense, chant, consecrated bread and wine, and communal participation. Churches are filled with icons of the First Ones, saints, martyrs, and heroes of the Sacrament. Citizens can often tell the time of day by the distant sound of liturgical chant.

The Church teaches, "Mercy and service are the highest offerings." Animal sacrifice is unknown. Communion uses consecrated bread and consecrated wine as an expression of unity, remembrance, covenant, and communion with the First Ones.

### REL-009 — Public Theology of the Great Six

Public theology is what ordinary people believe, not the hidden cosmology. Most people believe the First Ones, or Angels, created the world, shaped the mortal peoples, still guide humanity, and may answer prayer through miracles.

The Iron Church emphasizes the Great Six: Michael protects, Gabriel understands, Luke builds, John dreams, Raphael heals, and Uriel remembers. Humanity was created to dream. The faithful believe death does not end service.

### REL-010 — Michael, Public Theology

Michael is the Protector, Sword of Heaven, and Guardian of Humanity. His domains are protection, divine combat, courage, justice, vigilance, duty, and sacrifice. His symbol is a flaming sword. In Kaltheim, almost everyone prays to Michael for protection.

### REL-011 — Gabriel, Public Theology

Gabriel is the Revealer, Divine Voice, Herald of Truth, and Open Eye. Gabriel is associated with wisdom, knowledge, revelation, truth, dreams, and understanding. Scholars, judges, diplomats, teachers, investigators, students, and many Inquisitors pray to Gabriel.

### REL-012 — Luke, Public Theology

Luke is the Architect, Ordered Mind, Divine Craftsman, and Patron of Industry. Luke governs methodology, order, science, engineering, industry, tactics, logic, and disciplined thought. His symbol is a compass and hammer. Kaltheimian technological philosophy fits naturally under Luke: labor honors Creation.

### REL-013 — John, Public Theology

John is the Dreamer, Inspired One, Flame of Creation, First Poet, and Light of Inspiration. John governs creativity, art, music, poetry, imagination, storytelling, and innovation. His defining teaching is simple: humanity was created to dream.

### REL-014 — Raphael, Public Theology

Raphael is the Healer, Bearer of Mercy, Gentle Hand, and Guardian of the Afflicted. Raphael governs healing, mercy, compassion, charity, forgiveness, restoration, and protection of the weak. Hospitals throughout Kaltheim are commonly Church-operated under Raphael's patronage.

### REL-015 — Uriel, Public Theology

Uriel is Keeper of Memory, the Archivist, the Eternal Witness, the Rememberer, and the Silent Scribe. Uriel governs memory, history, record keeping, ancestry, scholarship, and preservation. The public reveres Uriel without knowing the full tragedy of his fracture.

### NAT-011 — Kaltheim-Veloria Relations

Kaltheim and Veloria are allies because they have to be, not because they like one another.

Their alliance was not born from easy friendship. It was forged because the Imperial League became too dangerous for either nation to face alone. The relationship resembles the wartime cooperation of opposed powers forced together by a greater threat: once the enemy was defeated, old distrust immediately resurfaced.

**Kaltheim's Neutrality**

Kaltheim's initial reluctance to enter foreign conflicts is consistent with its identity. Kaltheim is not isolationist because it fears war. It is isolationist because it has spent millennia preserving humanity, guarding sacred inheritances, and deciding very carefully when its strength should be unleashed.

To Kaltheim, foreign wars are not automatically Kaltheim's concern. They become Kaltheim's concern when they threaten humanity, Creation, or the survival of civilization itself.

**The Uneasy Alliance**

Kaltheim and Veloria differ in nearly every visible way.

Kaltheim is traditional. Veloria is modern.

Kaltheim is religious. Veloria is secular.

Kaltheim speaks in the language of duty. Veloria speaks in the language of liberty.

Kaltheim is disciplined, cautious, and communitarian, placing duty over self. Veloria is informal, innovative, restless, and fiercely proud of individual initiative.

These differences do not make cooperation impossible. They make cooperation exhausting.

**Technology Sharing**

Kaltheim refuses to export its most advanced technologies because it believes such things are sacred inheritances, not ordinary commodities. Many of these arts descend from Ancient Kaltheim and survived only because Kaltheim preserved them through catastrophe, pilgrimage, and war.

Distrust of Veloria reinforces this policy, but it is not the whole reason. Kaltheim does not share these technologies with anyone. Veloria simply resents the refusal more openly than most.

Restricted technologies include military systems, advanced industrial techniques, sacred engineering, superior metallurgy, Consecrated construction methods, Exalted research, and relic technology.

**Smuggling and Espionage**

The black market thrives wherever official policy creates hunger. Criminal syndicates, corrupt officials, private industrialists, and foreign agents all seek Kaltheimian weapons, blueprints, machine tools, alloys, and damaged military parts.

Veloria officially condemns such smuggling.

Privately, many authorities do not look too closely.

Smuggling and espionage remain constant sources of tension. One major espionage incident became a defining moment in OTS history, though it should not be treated as the origin of OTS itself.

**Mutual Respect**

For all their frustration, neither nation dismisses the other.

Kaltheim privately acknowledges that without Veloria's industry, manpower, and willingness to fight overseas, the Great Continental War would have lasted longer and cost far more lives.

Veloria openly recognizes that without Kaltheim's military strength and technological superiority, the Imperial League may have conquered the continent.

Neither nation believes it could have won alone.

Neither nation enjoys admitting it.

**Common Sayings**

A Kaltheimian officer once summarized the relationship neatly:

"The Velorians are our closest allies. Creator willing, they shall remain at a safe distance."

A Velorian reply is just as famous:

"One day Kaltheim will learn to have fun. We're determined to help."

Neither side finds the other's joke amusing.

## Government

### GOV-001 — Government of the Sacrament of Kaltheim

The Sacrament is governed by a partnership between the Iron Church and the military: the Church preserves purpose, and the military preserves the nation. A common saying captures the arrangement: "The Synod guides. The Protector acts."

The High Synod handles doctrine, civil law, domestic policy, the Iron Church, Consecrators, monasteries, and confirmation of the Lord Protector. The Archon of the Vigil is the spiritual head of the Sacrament, chosen by the High Synod and serving until death or retirement. The Lord Protector is the highest secular and military office, supreme commander, defender of the Sacrament, executor of Synod policy, leader of foreign affairs, and holder of emergency authority.

The office of Lord Protector is not hereditary. Nobles exist, but merit and service matter more than birth. Every citizen receives military training, most enter the reserves, the standing army is professional, and nonmilitary service is equally honored. The civic question is always: "How will you serve?"

### GOV-002 — Government of the Commonwealth of Veloria

Veloria is a constitutional presidential republic with three independent branches. The President of the Commonwealth is Head of State, Head of Government, Commander-in-Chief, leader of foreign policy, executor of law, appointment authority, and emergency authority. The President is directly elected to a four-year term with a two-term limit.

The Assembly of Commons is the lower house, elected by districts, and controls taxation, public finance, ordinary legislation, and direct representation of the populace. The Senate represents states or provinces and handles treaties, appointments, legislative review, and regional interests. The High Court interprets the constitution, resolves intergovernmental disputes, conducts judicial review, and protects civil liberties.

OTS, the Office of Tactical Security, is constitutionally subordinate but has unusually broad discretionary authority, creating recurring debate over civil liberties, executive power, secrecy, and oversight.

Citizenship in Kaltheim is a sacred covenant of mutual obligation. Citizens owe service, loyalty, vigilance, and sacrifice; the Sacrament owes protection, justice, community, and purpose. Every citizen swears an adulthood oath to stand the Vigil and ensure the Sacrament does not fail through them.

## Technology

### TEC-001 — Kaltheimian Technology

Kaltheim possesses one of the most advanced industrial bases in the known world. Its achievements are founded almost entirely upon engineering, metallurgy, chemistry, physics, mechanics, and disciplined craftsmanship. Though deeply religious, Kaltheimian industry is overwhelmingly mechanical rather than magical.

The vast majority of Kaltheimian technology is mundane: railways, armored vehicles, aircraft, factories, electrical grids, radios, industrial machinery, precision firearms, steam engines, diesel engines, electrical power, precision machining, industrial chemistry, and large-scale manufacturing. These are not miracles. They are the products of generations of learning, labor, and disciplined engineering.

Technology itself is not sacred to Kaltheim. The Iron Church teaches that humanity was created with the capacity to build. Every bridge raised, engine assembled, field cultivated, and city constructed is viewed as humanity fulfilling the purpose entrusted to it by the First Ones. Kaltheimians say, "The machine is not holy. The hands that build it honor Creation."

Kaltheim draws a sharp distinction between divine miracles and arcane magic. Divine power is understood as a gift granted through the Communion and exercised in faithful service. Arcane magic is viewed with suspicion because many within the Iron Church believe it encourages dependence upon powers outside humanity's proper vocation, while engineering and craftsmanship cultivate discipline, cooperation, and stewardship. This is a dominant theological concern rather than an absolute condemnation. Kaltheimians say, "A machine built by ten thousand hands honors Creation. A spell cast by one mortal honors only himself."

Engineers, machinists, blacksmiths, architects, physicians, and industrial workers occupy places of great respect in Kaltheimian society. Their work is not considered miraculous. It is considered faithful. To build well is to serve well, and to serve well is to honor the Creator.

Kaltheim excels at heavy industry, metallurgy, engines, armor, military hardware, logistics, and mass production. Its philosophy reinforces one of Eismark's central themes: humanity's greatness comes not from effortless power, but from the patient work of building civilization together.

### TEC-002 — Velorian Technology

Where Kaltheim builds through centuries of preservation and refinement, Veloria advances through adaptation, experimentation, and innovation. Its industry combines conventional engineering with widespread arcane practice, creating technologies that are often ingenious, unconventional, and rapidly evolving.

Velorian engineering is driven less by tradition than by necessity. Engineers are encouraged to experiment, modify existing designs, solve problems creatively, and improve upon imported ideas. Velorians say, "If it works, improve it." Unlike Kaltheim, whose greatest technologies descend directly from Ancient Kaltheim, Veloria developed much of its industrial base independently after the Great Forgetting. When foreign technology is obtained, Velorian engineers rarely copy it exactly; they adapt it to suit their own needs.

Veloria places little distinction between engineering and arcane study. Magic is another discipline of knowledge, and engineers, artificers, and mages frequently collaborate. Arcane techniques are commonly integrated into manufacturing, communications, transportation, medicine, and industrial processes. Magic is treated as a practical tool rather than a theological danger.

Relations with Kaltheim are strained by disagreements over technological stewardship. Kaltheim believes many ancient technologies are dangerous inheritances that must be carefully preserved and controlled. Veloria argues that knowledge belongs to humanity, and that progress cannot flourish if one nation alone decides who is worthy to possess it. Many Velorians view Kaltheim's policies as paternalistic rather than malicious, summed up in the complaint, "They act like they're the only adults left in the world."

Modern Velorian firearms trace their origins to smuggled Kaltheimian technology. Criminal organizations acquired damaged weapons, industrial machinery, replacement components, technical drawings, and manufacturing techniques. Velorian engineers reverse-engineered these technologies, then developed firearms emphasizing craftsmanship, elegance, personalization, ease of modification, and user culture rather than Kaltheim's standardized military doctrine.

Velorian industry favors skilled workshops, private manufacturers, competitive innovation, independent inventors, and commercial production. Products often vary between manufacturers, and individual gunsmiths, machinists, and artificers are known for distinctive styles and improvements.

Approximately forty years ago, Veloria enacted the Firearms and Spirits Act, prohibiting civilian ownership of firearms and the production and sale of alcohol. The law was meant to reduce organized violence and improve public order. Instead, organized crime came to dominate the black market, illegal firearms became widespread, prohibition became inconsistently enforced, corruption became commonplace, and public confidence in the law steadily eroded. Military and law enforcement remain exempt.

Where Kaltheim teaches stewardship, Veloria teaches opportunity. Many Velorians believe technology should remain open to everyone capable of advancing it. Invention represents liberty, self-improvement, economic prosperity, and individual initiative. A machine is valued not because it preserves tradition, but because it makes tomorrow better than today.

Kaltheim builds machines intended to last centuries. Veloria builds machines intended to be improved next year. A common engineering joke says, "Kaltheim builds the perfect machine. We build the next one."

Neither nation is simply more advanced. Kaltheim excels at heavy industry, metallurgy, engines, armor, military hardware, logistics, and mass production. Veloria excels at miniaturization, consumer technology, communications, optics, chemical engineering, arcane engineering, and commercial innovation.

### MIL-011 — The Armed Forces of the Sacrament

The Armed Forces of the Sacrament are Kaltheim's professional military forces, organized not for conquest but for the sacred duty of vigilance. Their five principles are Vigilance, Discipline, Faith, Professionalism, and Overwhelming Force.

Centuries of warfare have shaped every aspect of Kaltheimian military doctrine. Kaltheim expects citizen service and broad military training, but its standing military is composed of career professionals. Kaltheimian soldiers are among the most disciplined and extensively trained troops in the world, and promotion is based primarily on competence, faith, leadership, experience, and character rather than birth or political influence.

Doctrine emphasizes the swift and total destruction of existential threats through combined arms warfare, superior logistics, heavy artillery, mechanized offensives, air superiority, divine support, fortress defense, sieges, trench warfare, and logistics-driven attrition. Every branch exists to support the others. No arm fights alone.

Overwhelming firepower does not mean reckless aggression. It means concentrated force, disciplined coordination, relentless pressure, and the methodical destruction of an enemy's ability to continue fighting. When rapid victory cannot be achieved, Kaltheim readily transitions to prolonged siege and entrenchment warfare, relying on superior engineering, logistics, and industrial capacity to exhaust the enemy. The Sacrament is comfortable fighting rapid armored offensives, grinding trench warfare, fortress defense, prolonged sieges, and logistical wars of attrition.

Faith is inseparable from military service. Priests serve as chaplains, healers, officers, and advisors. Paladins are integrated throughout the chain of command, with at least one Paladin expected per platoon.

The major branches are the Sacrosanct Army, Iron Fleet, Skyward Host, Consecrator Corps, and Templar Orders. The Sacrosanct Army handles infantry, armor, mechanized warfare, artillery, logistics, and ground operations. The Iron Fleet handles surface fleets, arctic operations, coastal defense, naval aviation, and maritime logistics. The Skyward Host handles fighters, bombers, airships, reconnaissance, strategic transport, and air superiority. The Consecrator Corps handles combat engineering, fortifications, recovery operations, industrial maintenance, and logistics infrastructure.

Their symbolism is simple: the Army holds the land; the Fleet guards the seas; the Host watches the heavens; the Corps builds and sustains; the Templar Orders preserve the soul.

Kaltheimians say, "There is no glory in a war that needlessly endures," and "Peace is our desire. Victory is our duty."

War is not sought for conquest, wealth, or purposeless glory. Many Kaltheimians regard it as a solemn burden. Soldiers, Templars, and members of the Iron Church may also believe that righteous war fought in faithful service is noble, even glorious, when waged in defense of the innocent and in accordance with the Creator's will. Kaltheim does not glorify conquest; it glorifies faithful service.

### SOC-002 — Kaltheim-Veloria Technology Dispute

Kaltheim possesses engineering knowledge, metallurgy, and sacred industrial techniques that no other nation can replicate.

It does not export them.

Not because Kaltheim believes itself greedy owner of the world's machines, but because it believes itself their steward.

To Kaltheim, many technologies are dangerous inheritances from Ancient Kaltheim, preserved at tremendous cost since the Great Pilgrimage. Unrestricted dissemination risks another civilizational catastrophe. These machines survived because Kaltheim protected them, and Kaltheim will not allow the world to misuse them.

Veloria sees the issue differently. To Velorians, technology belongs to humanity. Innovation should be shared. Knowledge should never be hoarded. Progress cannot survive if one nation decides who is worthy to learn.

Many Velorians do not see Kaltheim as evil. They see it as paternalistic.

"They act like they're the only adults left in the world."

**The Smuggling Trade**

Kaltheim exports only limited quantities of raw materials: steel, specialized alloys, and perhaps certain machine tools. It does not export military technology, sacred construction techniques, or advanced weapon designs.

So the black market fills the gap.

Criminal organizations risk enormous sums smuggling firearms, blueprints, machine tools, industrial components, replacement parts, and occasionally damaged Kaltheim weapons.

The Iron Church treats this as theft of sacred property. Veloria officially condemns it.

Unofficially, Velorian authorities often do not investigate very hard.

**The Birth of Velorian Firearms**

Veloria did not invent guns independently. It reverse-engineered them from stolen receivers, copied mechanisms, salvaged barrels, recovered blueprints, and experimental reproductions.

That history explains why Kaltheim's guns look standardized, military, and industrial, while Veloria's firearms are more handcrafted, individual, and beautiful.

Kaltheim invented the rifle. Veloria perfected making it beautiful.

**The Prohibition Spiral**

Kaltheim developed firearms. Smugglers stole examples. Velorian engineers copied them. Crime syndicates armed themselves first. The government panicked and outlawed firearms.

Then only criminals had guns.

Alcohol prohibition followed a similar reformist logic. Reformers argued that alcohol fueled organized crime and firearms fueled political violence, so both were prohibited. Instead, organized crime became richer, public frustration grew, and enforcement became inconsistent.

No one is completely right.

Kaltheim is not selfish. It has preserved dangerous knowledge for centuries and genuinely fears misuse.

Veloria is not reckless. It is frustrated that one nation claims authority over what all humanity is allowed to learn.

That is why the dispute cuts so deep.

## Law

### LAW-001 — The Public Safety and Temperance Act

Also known as the Firearms and Spirits Act.

The Public Safety and Temperance Act is one of the most controversial laws in modern Veloria. Enacted approximately forty years before the campaign, it prohibits civilian firearm ownership, civilian firearm manufacture, and the production and sale of alcoholic beverages. Though still law, the Act is now widely ignored, selectively enforced, and viewed by much of the public as a failure.

**Canon Facts**

- The Act was enacted approximately forty years before the campaign.
- It prohibits civilian ownership of firearms.
- It prohibits civilian manufacture of firearms.
- It prohibits the production, sale, and commercial distribution of alcoholic beverages.
- Military personnel and law enforcement agencies are exempt.
- The law remains officially in force.

**Intended Purpose**

Veloria justified the law as a public safety measure. It was meant to promote social stability and reduce organized violence.

It did not.

**Consequences**

- Organized crime expanded dramatically.
- Firearms smuggling became one of the republic's most profitable black-market industries.
- Illegal distilleries and speakeasies became commonplace.
- Corruption spread throughout local and national government.
- Public confidence in the government steadily declined.

Officially, civilian firearm ownership is illegal and alcohol remains prohibited. Unofficially, many citizens possess illegal firearms, criminal syndicates openly traffic weapons, entire neighborhoods ignore prohibition, and speakeasies operate throughout Veloria.

Enforcement varies dramatically depending on location and political influence.

Common illegal firearms include rotators, slugthrowers, barkers, and longstrikes.

**Government Corruption**

Many public officials continue to support the Act in public while violating it in private. Some accept bribes from smuggling organizations. Others possess illegal firearms, protect criminal enterprises, facilitate weapons trafficking, or quietly purchase military equipment through corrupt channels.

This hypocrisy has become one of the defining political scandals of the republic.

**Public Opinion**

Most Velorians believe the Act has failed.

Two common sayings capture the mood:

"The law only disarmed the people who followed it."

"If the government won't protect us, we'll protect ourselves."

As strange and increasingly dangerous phenomena begin appearing across Eismark, public support for repealing the Act continues to grow.

**International Perspective**

Kaltheim views the Act as evidence of Veloria's political weakness. Among Kaltheim's leadership, it is commonly argued that a nation unable to enforce its own laws cannot be trusted with advanced military technologies.

This perception further reinforces Kaltheim's longstanding refusal to export its most advanced weapons, engineering, and sacred technologies to Veloria.

### LAW-002 — Kaltheimian Justice System

Kaltheimian justice rests on the belief that law derives from doctrine. Civic courts are led by Ecclesiastical Magistrates. Military tribunals handle soldiers. Ecclesiastical Courts handle heresy, sedition, corruption, doctrine, and existential threats.

### CAM-002 — The Bar

The Bar is not a fixed place. It has no permanent location, no permanent appearance, and no permanent name. Yet those who have found it all agree on one thing.

It is simply The Bar.

It is not an inn in the ordinary sense. It is not a magical safe house waiting to be found on a map. It is a sanctuary for the forgotten, manifested through one fragment of the Angel of Memory.

**The Exterior**

No two appearances are identical. The Bar always manifests in a form appropriate to its surroundings.

It might appear as a warm jazz speakeasy in Veloria, tucked behind rain-slick brick and amber glass. In Kaltheim, it may be a stone mountain tavern with frost on the lintel and firelight beneath the door. On the frontier, it could be a lonely roadside inn, a weathered harbor pub, or an isolated wilderness lodge where no building stood the night before.

The architecture changes. The feeling never does.

**The Sign**

Every visitor remembers seeing a sign above the entrance. Yet no two people remember the same name.

One traveler recalls The Rusty Nail. Another swears it was The Wayfarer's Rest. Another remembers only The Hearth. When compared later, nobody can explain the discrepancy. Eventually, everyone simply calls it The Bar.

**Recognition**

Those who have visited The Bar never mistake it for another building. Regardless of its appearance, they know immediately what it is.

No explanation is required. No one questions it. A simple glance is enough.

**The Bartender**

No matter where The Bar appears, The Bartender is always there.

He may wear different clothing. The furniture may change. The room itself may be entirely different. Yet he is always found quietly cleaning a glass, welcoming weary travelers, remembering people he should not remember, while never remembering himself.

**The Interior**

Though The Bar's appearance changes, certain things never do. It is always warm, safe, welcoming, and strangely familiar.

The music may differ. The food may differ. The architecture may differ. But every visitor experiences the same sense of refuge.

**Rules of the House**

- No violence is permitted. Guests are expected to speak truthfully, respect the dead, and understand that everyone eventually leaves.
- Living and dead may briefly meet there.
- Visitors have a usual drink, and the Bartender knows it.

**Departure**

The Bar cannot be intentionally revisited. Once a visitor leaves, it disappears. Returning moments later reveals only an ordinary road, an empty field, an abandoned building, or untouched wilderness.

No known method exists to locate The Bar by choice.

**Other Witnesses**

The Bar is not unique to the player characters. Throughout history, scattered accounts describe impossible taverns appearing during moments of great hardship.

An old veteran may remark, "Funny thing. During the war I found a little tavern in the middle of nowhere. Never could find it again."

A dying priest may whisper, "Tell the bartender... thank you."

Most dismiss these stories as coincidence. Few realize they describe the same place.

**Why The Bar Appears**

No one knows why The Bar appears when it does.

Many believe it comes to those who are lost. Not merely lost upon the road, but lost within themselves. Whether this is the will of Uriel, the instinct of one of his surviving fragments, or something else entirely remains unknown.

The Bar should not be treated as a place consciously dispatched by the Communion. It is better understood as a natural expression of Uriel's fragmented nature: the Angel of Memory, broken and scattered, still offering refuge to those who have lost their way.

The Bar never appears when the players need shelter. It appears when they need to remember who they are.

The Bar remembers what the world has forgotten.

**The Bar Remembers**

The Bar is not static.

As the characters recover lost memories and draw nearer to the truth, The Bar subtly changes. Early in the campaign it feels almost impossibly quiet. Nearly empty. Almost untouched.

As the campaign progresses, more patrons appear. Music returns. Laughter fills the room. Photographs decorate the walls. Forgotten keepsakes appear upon shelves. Old stories are told once more.

Eventually the characters notice something unsettling. Many of these objects belong to them. Some depict moments they cannot remember. Others show companions they have never met.

Or so they believe.

The Bar remembers what they have forgotten.

It also evolves with hope. Early visits may feel empty, cold, sparsely lit, and nearly patronless. Later, the room may hold music, conversation, a warm fireplace, old veterans trading stories, children laughing, and families reunited across the boundaries of memory and death.

In the late campaign, the Bartender may even smile.

The Bar is not merely remembering. Uriel is healing.

**GM Guidance**

The Bar should never become routine. Its appearances should remain rare.

Ideally, The Bar appears after major revelations, during moments of profound uncertainty, following great sacrifice, or when the characters are emotionally lost. Its rarity preserves its mystery.

**See Also:** NPC-003 — The Bartender; COS-006 — The Communion; CAM-001 — Vein of Eismark Memory Premise; CAP-015 — The Bar; CAP-016 — Afterlife Rules

### CAM-003 — The Abandoned One

The Abandoned One is not the campaign's opening assignment. No king, council, church, or ministry begins the story by sending the characters to find it. Its existence is a buried truth, something the world has forgotten so thoroughly that even the surviving records do not understand what they are pointing toward.

At the table, The Abandoned One should emerge as a revelation.

The characters may first encounter contradictions: a Joivian that should have marched but did not, a record that names Uriel where no name should remain, a battlefield account that remembers one more holy machine than history permits, or a memory fragment that feels older than the person remembering it. Piece by piece, those clues should lead toward the realization that one Joivian survived the march into the Great Maw.

That survivor is The Abandoned One: the Joivian containing Uriel's vessel.

**Campaign Role**

- It connects Uriel's fracture to the Great Forgetting.
- It anchors the lost history of the Joivians in something the characters can eventually find.
- It turns the memory premise from atmosphere into plot.
- It gives the campaign a hidden survivor rather than a known target.
- It should be discovered through exploration, ruins, recovered history, and memory fragments.

**Design Notes**

- This entry is the campaign-facing version of The Abandoned One.
- NPC-002 remains the identity and lore entry.
- Project Resurgence has been reopened as an under-review campaign concept in CAM-004 and should not be treated as locked canon until the campaign status conflict is resolved.
- Future revisions can add clue chains, reveal stages, encounter framing, and what the players can learn at different points in the campaign.

### CAM-004 — Project Resurgence

**Status:** Under Review
**Classification:** Restricted - Council of Vigilance

Project Resurgence exists for one purpose: to locate and reactivate a surviving Joivian.

As signs of renewed corruption begin appearing throughout Eismark, the Council of Vigilance secretly concludes that the Veins may be reopening. Recovered fragments of First Age scripture imply an unsettling possibility: the Veins were not sealed through the sacrifice of the First Ones alone. The Divine Engines known in some restricted or older records as the `Jovians` may also have been required.

Current formal canon spelling remains `Joivian`; `Jovian` is preserved here as restricted or archive terminology.

If the Veins reopen, humanity possesses no known means of sealing them unless a Joivian can be found.

If this version is promoted to canon, the search for The Abandoned One is no longer absent from the world. It is hidden. Kaltheim secretly funds the work, burying it beneath deniable archaeological missions, military excavations, and recovery expeditions that officially do not exist.

The project sends people into places most nations would rather forget: ancient battlefields, glacial depths, sealed ruins, and First Age vaults whose names have been scraped out of history. Publicly, these expeditions are academic, military, or logistical operations. Privately, they are part of a single effort to find something holy, ancient, and possibly still alive.

**Project Objective**

- Locate a surviving Joivian.
- Reactivate a surviving Joivian.
- Determine whether it can be reactivated.
- Recover forgotten First Age records, vaults, or components tied to Joivian survival.
- Keep the operation deniable.

**Secretly Funded Operations**

- Archaeological expeditions.
- Military excavations.
- Expeditions into ancient battlefields.
- Exploration beneath glaciers.
- Recovery of forgotten First Age vaults.

**Consistency Note**

This reopens material previously preserved as ARC-017. It creates a campaign-structure question: should The Abandoned One be discovered with no organized search underway, or should the characters slowly uncover that Kaltheim and the Council of Vigilance have been searching in secret all along?

The cleanest current reconciliation is that no public search exists, while Project Resurgence operates in secret. This keeps the mystery intact for most of the world while allowing Kaltheim to have a hidden, deniable hand in the search.

**Classification:** Restricted - Council of Vigilance

Project Resurgence was an early campaign concept for Vein of Eismark, now reopened under CAM-004. In that version of the story, a secret initiative by the Council of Vigilance sought to locate and reactivate a surviving Joivian. The world does not know that a surviving Joivian remains. No public search exists. The mystery can still emerge from the campaign: old records that do not agree, battlefields that remember too much, ruins that should be empty, and the gradual realization that the last survivor of the Joivians was not merely lost.

This preserves the mystery surrounding the Joivians' march into the Great Maw and makes the eventual revelation of The Abandoned One far more powerful.
