# Vein of Eismark -- Canon Lore Summary

## Setting

- **World:** Vardheim
- **Continent:** Eismark
- **Campaign:** Vein of Eismark

The setting is a dieselpunk/cosmic horror world where ancient divine history, forgotten truths, and an incomprehensible extradimensional force threaten reality itself.

---

# The First Age

In the beginning there were the **First Ones**, later remembered as the Angels.

They were sent into Vardheim with one purpose:

> **Create.**

The First Ones seeded the mortal races in their own image.

- Human Angels created Humans.
- Orc Angels created Orcs.
- Dragon Angels created Dragons.
- Other First Ones created the remaining races.

The races originally lived together under Angelic guidance.

---

## Kaltheim

The Angels and Humanity together founded:

> **Kaltheim, the First City**

During the First Age, Kaltheim was:

- A city of learning.
- A center of philosophy and science.
- A beacon of cooperation.

Knowledge was shared freely.

This era is remembered as:

> **The Age of Illumination**

---

# The Great First Ones

Many Angels existed, but six Human First Ones became the foremost teachers of civilization.

### Michael
**Angel of Protection and Divine War**

Taught:
- Protection
- Duty
- Sacrifice
- Stewardship

Modern Kaltheim worship centers primarily on Michael, though his teachings have become heavily distorted.

---

### Gabriel
**Angel of Divine Understanding**

Taught:
- Mathematics
- Astronomy
- Philosophy
- Reality studies

---

### Luke
**Angel of Methodology**

Taught:
- Engineering
- Scientific process
- Medicine
- Systems

---

### John
**Angel of Creativity**

Taught:
- Art
- Architecture
- Invention

---

### Raphael
**Angel of Healing**

Taught:
- Healing
- Agriculture
- Mercy

---

### Uriel
**Angel of Memory**

Taught:
- History
- Language
- Preservation

Uriel was more than an Angel of Memory.

He was:

> **The Memory of the Communion itself.**

---

# The First Veins

Scholars of Kaltheim discovered:

> Reality was not singular.

Vardheim was only one world among countless others.

This discovery attracted the attention of something beyond reality.

Soon, fractures appeared in existence.

These fractures became known as:

> **Veins**

Through them seeped a substance called:

> **The Blood**

---

# The Blood

The Blood is not alive.

It is not dead.

It is not malicious.

Wherever it manifests:

- Reality destabilizes.
- Life mutates.
- Matter changes.
- Bloodspawn are created.

The Blood is effectively contamination caused by contact between incompatible realities.

---

# The Entity Beyond the Veins

An incomprehensible extradimensional existence exists beyond the Veins.

Important truths:

- It is **not malicious**.
- It is fundamentally **unknowable**.
- It cannot be reasoned with.
- It never speaks directly.
- It has no known form.
- It should never be fully explained.

Mortals experience it through:

- dreams
- symbols
- impossible experiences
- contradictions
- altered memories

The closest mortal approximation of its behavior is:

> curiosity

Though even this may be incorrect.

The Entity and Creation cannot coexist.

Contact between them inevitably damages reality.

---

# The Sacrifice of the First Ones

The Angels fought the corruption for centuries.

Eventually they concluded:

> The Veins could not be closed through force.

The First Ones gathered within Kaltheim.

Together they sacrificed nearly all of their divine essence.

Reality was stitched closed.

The Veins sealed.

The Blood ceased flowing.

The cost:

- Many Angels became dormant.
- Many became statues of white stone.
- Others descended beneath Kaltheim into eternal sleep.

Their final command:

> **Remain vigilant.**

---

# The Communion

The dormant First Ones remain linked through:

> **The Communion**

The Communion is a dreamlike collective consciousness.

It communicates through:

- dreams
- symbols
- emotions
- fragmented memories

Not direct conversation.

The Communion is the source of:

- Cleric magic
- Paladin miracles

Dreams are a central theme of the setting.

Dreams are rare and always meaningful.

---

# The Long Vigil

Though the Veins were sealed, Bloodspawn remained.

Civilization collapsed.

Only Kaltheim endured.

For tens of thousands of years, Kaltheim fought:

> **The Lingering Wars**

against the remnants of corruption.

---

# The Jovians

To fight the Bloodspawn, Kaltheim created:

> **Jovians**

Divine Engines of War.

Each Jovian contained a dormant Angel within its core.

They were:

- part machine
- part relic
- part cathedral

The Jovians became the turning point of the Lingering Wars.

---

# The Last March

Near the end of the Lingering Wars:

Every surviving Jovian stopped simultaneously.

No orders were given.

No explanation was provided.

All Jovians turned north and marched toward the greatest Vein ever known:

> **The World Wound**

Thousands entered the Vein.

The World Wound closed.

The Bloodspawn began dying.

The war ended.

No Jovian ever returned.

Only the Angels know what happened.

No mortal knows the truth.

---

# The Abandoned One

One Jovian never joined the Last March.

During the war, the Angel within became corrupted through direct exposure to the Blood.

Realizing the corruption threatened the Communion, the Angel severed itself from the collective.

Cut off from the Communion, it never heard the final call.

It remained behind.

Forgotten.

This being is now known only as:

> **The Abandoned One**

---

# Uriel

The Angel within the Abandoned One is:

> **Uriel, Angel of Memory**

Uriel severing himself had catastrophic consequences.

Because Uriel was the Memory of the Communion itself:

- The Angels began forgetting.
- Humanity began forgetting.
- Kaltheim began forgetting.

Over thousands of years:

- history fragmented
- religion drifted
- doctrine changed
- meaning was lost

Modern Kaltheim has forgotten most of its true history.

---

# Modern Kaltheim

Modern Kaltheim remembers only war.

It no longer remembers:

- why the Vigil began
- what the Blood truly was
- what the Jovians truly were

The only surviving link to the First Age is religion.

Kaltheim primarily worships:

> **Michael**

However, Michael's teachings have become heavily distorted through millennia of forgotten history.

Modern Kaltheim is:

- militaristic
- disciplined
- collectivist
- isolationist

It sees itself as civilization's final guardian.

# Uriel, the Rememberer
## Complete Canon Lore Summary

---

# Identity

**Uriel** is one of the **First Ones**, among the greatest beings created by the Creator.

He is known by the titles:

- **The Rememberer**
- **Keeper of Histories**

His divine domains are:

- **Memory**
- **History**
- **Preservation**
- **Language**
- **Truth**

Uriel does **not** directly create any known mortal race.

---

# Role in Creation

At the dawn of Creation:

- The Creator creates the First Ones.
- Uriel is among them.
- Together with the other First Ones and hundreds of lesser angels, Uriel helps shape Creation and guide the early world.

From the beginning, Uriel serves as:

> The living embodiment of collective memory.

He is not merely associated with memory.

He **is** Memory.

---

# The Long Vigil

When:

- The Entity discovers Creation,
- The Veins open,
- The Blood begins pouring into reality,

Uriel fights alongside:

- the First Ones,
- the lesser angels,
- and mortal civilizations.

This era becomes known as:

> **The Long Vigil.**

Eventually the angels realize the Veins cannot be permanently closed through force.

---

# Creation of the Communion

The angels collectively enter dormancy.

Many become statues.

Through their sacrifice they create:

> **The Communion**

The Communion seals the Veins and prevents the Entity from fully entering Creation.

Uriel becomes part of the Communion.

---

# Uriel's Search for Truth

At some point after the creation of the Communion:

> Uriel deliberately attempts to make contact with the Entity.

His reasons are unknown.

Possible motives may include:

- understanding,
- knowledge,
- truth,
- or discovering the nature of the enemy.

No definitive motive has yet been canonized.

Through this contact Uriel receives:

> forbidden truths.

The information slowly begins to corrupt him.

---

# The Fracture

Realizing that his corruption threatens the entirety of the Communion:

> Uriel voluntarily fractures himself.

He willingly leaves the Communion in order to save it.

This act is one of the greatest sacrifices in Eismark's history.

---

# The Great Forgetting

Because Uriel is the embodiment of collective memory:

> His departure unintentionally causes the Great Forgetting.

The Great Forgetting is therefore:

- not a punishment,
- not an attack,
- not a conspiracy.

It is:

> collateral damage caused by Uriel's sacrifice.

The world literally loses part of its ability to remember.

As a result:

- First Age history is largely lost.
- Second Age history becomes myth.
- Countless records vanish.
- Entire civilizations fade from memory.

---

# Uriel and the Joivians

During the Lingering War:

- Ancient Kaltheim becomes the last great beacon of mortal civilization.
- As a final hope, Ancient Kaltheim creates the **Joivians**.

One Joivian is unique.

> Uriel's dormant angelic vessel/statue is used in its construction.

---

# The Sleeper

Unlike the other Joivians:

> The Joivian containing Uriel does not march into the Great Maw.

Instead:

- it is left behind,
- dormant,
- alone.

A fragment of Uriel remains within it.

That fragment:

- cries out,
- cannot remember who it is,
- exists in profound loneliness.

The being who once embodied memory can no longer remember even itself.

Its current location is unknown.

This entity is sometimes informally referred to as:

> **The Sleeper.**

---

# The Bartender

Not all of Uriel leaves the Communion.

A surviving fragment remains.

This fragment becomes:

> **The Bartender**

The Bartender resides within:

> **The Bar**

The Bar exists inside the Communion.

Individuals may reach the Bar through:

- dreams,
- near-death experiences,
- spiritual journeys,
- unknown means.

---

# The Bar

The Bar's rules are simple:

1. All are welcome.
2. No violence.
3. Speak truthfully.
4. Respect the dead.
5. Everyone eventually leaves.

---

# The Bartender's Nature

The Bartender is:

- kind,
- tired,
- patient,
- quietly humorous,
- melancholy,
- incomplete.

He knows:

> He is a fragment of Uriel.

He remembers only pieces of what Uriel once was.

Most horrifying of all:

> He can forget.

The Bartender embodies:

- memory,
- hospitality,
- mercy,
- loss,
- hope.

---

# Uriel's Testament

**Uriel's Testament** is an organization devoted to recovering lost truth.

Its members include:

- scholars,
- explorers,
- archivists,
- historians,
- truth-seekers.

They investigate:

- ancient ruins,
- First Age mysteries,
- Second Age mysteries,
- lost records,
- dangerous truths,
- the Great Forgetting.

Some members are reckless.

**Professor Kath** has worked alongside them but opposes their more extreme elements.

For **Vein of Eismark**, I'd divide the lore into **three tiers**, not two.

# Tier I -- Public Knowledge (Player Starting Knowledge)

This is what an educated person in Vardheim knows.

## Vardheim

- The world is called **Vardheim**.
- The continent is **Eismark**.
- The current major powers are **Kaltheim** and **Veloria**.

## Kaltheim

- Isolationist mountain theocracy.
- Highly militarized and authoritarian.
- Worships Michael.
- Restricts advanced technology.
- Uses Divine magic exclusively.
- Refuses to share most technological advancements.

## Veloria

- Republic founded under the Founding Compact.
- Values liberty, commerce, and individualism.
- Arcane magic is common.
- Suffers from corruption and organized crime.
- The Firearms and Spirits Act exists but is widely ignored.

## The White Hand

- Smuggling syndicates operating between Kaltheim and Veloria.
- Known for trafficking guns, alcohol, and contraband.

## The OTS

- Veloria's intelligence and internal security service.
- Publicly exists to protect Velorian interests and enforce federal law.

## Religion

- Most of humanity worships Michael in some form.
- The Iron Church dominates Kaltheim.
- Veloria possesses a much looser religious tradition.

## History

Everyone knows:

- The world suffered a terrible ancient catastrophe.
- Humanity survived because of the Vigil.
- Nobody knows exactly what the Vigil was.
