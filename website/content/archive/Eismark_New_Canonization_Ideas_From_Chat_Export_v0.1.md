### IDEA-001 -- Nordic / Harsh Frontier

Signal Words: save this

Candidate Idea:

For **Vein of Kaltheim**, I'd aim for something that feels **old, cold, and slightly mythic**--a name that can support both a dieselpunk world war and creeping cosmic horror.

Here are some options grouped by tone:

### Nordic / Harsh Frontier
- **Vardheim** -- "World of Watchers." Grim and northern.
- **Skeldr** -- Short, harsh, and memorable.
- **Norrvein** -- "Northern Vein"; ties into your campaign title.
- **Eisgard** -- "Ice Realm"; simple and evocative.
- **Dravik** -- Rugged, industrial feel.

### Mythic / Cosmic Horror
- **Astraeon** -- Ancient and grand, with subtle cosmic undertones.
- **Eldrath** -- Sounds old and slightly forbidden.
- **Vaelmyr** -- Melancholic and mythic.
- **Threnos** -- Evokes tragedy and old empires.
- **Nythgard** -- "Night Realm"; fits eldritch themes.

### Dieselpunk / Industrial
- **Karos** -- Strong, concise, easy to remember.
- **Mordane** -- Industrial and grim without sounding overtly evil.
- **Brask** -- Harsh, mechanical, almost Slavic/Nordic.
- **Vespera** -- Elegant, works well if your setting has many competing city-states.
- **Aethyr** -- Suggests both industry and something unnatural beneath reality.

### My recommendations for your setting:

1. **Vardheim** -- My favorite. It immediately conjures icy mountains, old kingdoms, and a harsh world.
2. **Threnos** -- Excellent if you want a world defined by endless war and ancient sorrow.
3. **Astraeon** -- Best if the cosmic horror aspect will become central.
4. **Karos** -- Short, punchy, easy for players to remember.
5. **Nythgard** -- Great for a dark, eldritch military setting.

Example:

> **World:** *Vardheim*  
> **Continent:** *Eismark*  
> **Campaign:** *Vein of Kaltheim*

or

> **World:** *Astraeon*  
> **Continent:** *Vardheim*  
> **Campaign:** *Vein of Kaltheim*

I think **World: Vardheim** paired with **Campaign: Vein of Kaltheim** has the strongest overall identity for ALT 1948.

### IDEA-003 -- Proposed Canon

Signal Words: save this

Candidate Idea:

Yes.

This is the missing piece.

Make it canon.

It ties together almost every major theme in the setting.

Current themes:

- Dreams
- Memory
- Isolation
- Forgotten history
- Communion
- Corruption

This idea unifies them.

# Proposed Canon

Uriel was not merely:

> The Angel of Memory.

He was:

> **The Memory of the Communion itself.**

Every First One could remember individually, but Uriel acted as the anchor that preserved the collective memory of the Angels.

When Uriel severed himself from the Communion, he unknowingly took part of Creation's memory with him.

The immediate consequences were subtle.

Over centuries:

- memories faded
- records were lost
- meanings changed
- doctrine drifted
- history fragmented

The First Ones themselves began forgetting.

Then mankind forgot.

Then Kaltheim forgot.

The world slowly descended into amnesia.

This is incredibly tragic because:

> Uriel isolated himself to save the Communion.

And in doing so, doomed it to forget.

Even better:

The Angels may know this.

Michael may know this.

But the dormant Angels cannot explain it.

They only dream:

> **"Find Uriel."**

# Additional Consequences

## The Iron Church

The Church unknowingly exists because of Uriel's absence.

Michael's teachings became distorted because:

> Nobody remembered them correctly.

The original Michael taught:

- protection
- sacrifice
- stewardship

Modern Michael teaches:

- vigilance
- strength
- war

The transformation happened gradually over ten thousand years.

Nobody noticed.

## Kaltheim

Kaltheim did not lose its records.

Kaltheim lost its ability to remember what the records meant.

A historian may possess a First Age text and still be unable to comprehend it fully.

The context is gone.

## The Communion

The Communion itself is wounded.

Dreams are fragmented.

Memories are incomplete.

The dormant Angels frequently confuse:

- people
- places
- eras

Michael may dream of a soldier and call him by the name of someone dead for twenty thousand years.

# Campaign Payoff

Purifying Uriel does not simply save an Angel.

It heals the Communion.

For the first time in ten thousand years:

The First Ones remember.

The consequences should be enormous.

Examples:

- dormant Angels begin dreaming clearly
- forgotten vaults open
- ancient technologies reactivate
- miracles become stronger
- lost histories return

Most importantly:

Michael finally remembers what happened to the Jovians.

That should be one of the biggest revelations in the entire campaign.

This is one of the strongest ideas you've had for the setting so far. It should absolutely be canon.

### IDEA-004 -- Rule 1: The Entity cannot be reasoned with.

Signal Words: save this

Candidate Idea:

Agreed. That's the correct approach.

The Entity should never become a character in the traditional sense.

The players should never be able to say:

> "The Entity wants X."

At best, they should be able to say:

> "The Entity appears to be curious."

or

> "The Entity seems to seek understanding."

Even those conclusions should be tentative.

I would establish a few hard rules.

# Rule 1: The Entity cannot be reasoned with.

Not because it is hostile.

Because communication itself breaks down.

Imagine an ant trying to explain morality to a human.

Now increase the gulf by several orders of magnitude.

---

# Rule 2: The Entity does not understand Creation.

Fundamental concepts may be completely alien to it.

Examples:

- death
- sacrifice
- individuality
- time
- memory
- purpose

It may ask:

> "Why do you end*"

or

> "Why are you many*"

without understanding that the questions are horrifying.

---

# Rule 3: The Entity is not omniscient.

This is important.

Curiosity only works if it genuinely does not know things.

The Entity should learn.

Observe.

Experiment.

Wonder.

---

# Rule 4: The Entity should never directly explain itself.

No monologues.

No:

> "I come from the Seventh Dimension."

No:

> "My goal is..."

Nothing that concrete.

Everything should come through:

- dreams
- symbolism
- impossible imagery
- fragmented impressions

---

# Rule 5: The Entity's motives should remain fundamentally unknowable.

The players can infer patterns.

They can never confirm intentions.

Example:

The Entity repeatedly opens Veins near population centers.

Question:

Is it:

- seeking intelligence*
- seeking emotion*
- seeking memory*
- seeking worship*
- simply following concentrations of life*
- doing something completely unrelated*

Nobody knows.

---

One thing I would add:

The Entity should be as incapable of understanding humanity as humanity is of understanding it.

This creates a nice symmetry.

**Humanity:**

> "Why is it doing this*"

**The Entity:**

> "Why do they fear me*"

Neither side can truly answer.

That is where the tragedy lives.

Final criticism:

Do not ever reveal the Entity's true form.

Ever.

Not to the players.

Not to the Angels.

Not even to yourself.

The moment you define it completely, it stops being cosmic horror and becomes a monster.

### IDEA-005 -- You're right. The line:

Signal Words: save this

Candidate Idea:

You're right. The line:

> "Why do they fear me*"

is too human.

Fear implies that the Entity:

1. Understands emotions.
2. Understands other minds.
3. Cares about the answer.

You haven't established any of those.

A better framework is:

The Entity notices patterns.

That's it.

Examples:

- Contact occurs.
- Organisms cease functioning.
- Reality changes.
- Veins form.
- Dreams propagate.

The Entity observes correlations but may not understand causation.

Even "curiosity" is probably an approximation made by mortals.

The Angels might say:

> *"It seeks understanding."*

But that could simply be the closest concept they possess.

A stronger version:

> The Entity exhibits behavior consistent with observation, experimentation, and repeated contact.
>
> Whether this constitutes curiosity is unknown.

The key is that the Entity should not think in language, emotions, desires, or intentions recognizable to mortals.

The Angels themselves should struggle to describe it.

For example, Gabriel or Uriel might say:

> *"I do not believe it hated us."*
>
> *"I do not believe it loved us."*
>
> *"I am no longer certain those concepts apply."*

That's the level you want.

One hard rule I'd establish:

**The Entity never speaks directly.**

No dialogue.

No telepathy.

No "messages."

Only:

- dreams
- impressions
- symbols
- impossible experiences
- altered memories
- contradictions

The moment the players can have a conversation with it, the horror diminishes dramatically.

### IDEA-009 -- The Awakening

Signal Words: save it

I think this is very close, but it needs one tweak.

Right now the sequence is:

> Vein touches PCs → Entity contacts them → Communion notices → amnesia.

## The Awakening

Each PC, independently, encountered a newly forming Vein sometime before the campaign.

None remember the circumstances.

The encounters were all different:

- a miner uncovered something underground,
- a soldier found an impossible battlefield,
- a smuggler crossed the wrong mountain pass,
- a scholar opened a forbidden text.

The details are left to the players and revealed over time.

What matters is:

> **Each of them looked into a Vein.**
>
> **And something looked back.**

---

## The Dreams

Following this encounter, the PCs began experiencing dreams.

Not messages.

Not conversations.

Dreams.

Impossible dreams.

Dreams that lingered after waking.

Dreams that should not exist.

This is the Entity's "contact."

The Entity is not intentionally communicating.

The PCs simply began perceiving traces of it.

---

## The Communion Intervenes

The Communion notices.

This is extraordinarily unusual.

For the first time in millennia, mortals are experiencing persistent contact with something beyond Creation and surviving.

The Communion acts instinctively.

Not Michael.

Not an individual Angel.

The Communion itself.

Like an immune response.

It shelters the PCs within the Bar.

It fragments and suppresses their memories.

Not to harm them.

To protect them.

Because the Communion does not know:

> Whether the PCs are victims, carriers, or something else entirely.

---

## Why the PCs Are Important

This is the key.

The Communion's intervention had an unexpected side effect.

The PCs now exist between:

- remembrance and forgetting,
- dream and waking,
- the Entity and the Communion.

Because of this:

> **They can remember.**

Not perfectly.

Not immediately.

But unlike everyone else in Vardheim, they can recover lost memories.

Not just their own.

Potentially:

> the world's.

This is huge.

They may be able to:

- recover forgotten First Age knowledge,
- remember truths lost to Uriel's severance,
- restore memories to others,
- uncover things even the Angels have forgotten.

In a world built on forgetting, they are anomalies.

---

I especially like this line:

> **The Entity touched them. The Communion claimed them. Neither side understands why they survived.**

That's gas.

One final criticism:

I would never explain exactly **why** these specific people survived contact with a Vein.

Leave that unresolved.

Even Michael should not know.

That mystery will keep the players wondering for the entire campaign.

# Revised Canon: The Great Continental War

## The Imperial League

The Great Continental War began when a coalition of expansionist powers known collectively as:

> **The Imperial League**

began a campaign of conquest across the continent.

Their ideology was based on:

- expansion,
- militarism,
- authoritarianism,
- and the belief that weaker nations should be absorbed for the good of civilization.

Numerous smaller states fell.

The war rapidly escalated.

---

# Kaltheim's Position

Kaltheim initially remained neutral.

The High Synod and Lord Protector both believed:

> the League would eventually threaten the Sacrament.

However, Kaltheim also believed:

> it could defeat the League alone if necessary.

Many Kaltheimians viewed the conflict as:

> yet another foreign war.

The League eventually attacked Kaltheim's allies and violated several long-standing treaties.

Kaltheim entered the war.

The results were:

> catastrophic for the League.

---

# Valoria Intervenes

The Commonwealth of Valoria had no intention of allowing Kaltheim to face the League alone.

Officially, Valoria entered the war because:

- the League threatened global stability,
- trade,
- and democracy.

Unofficially:

many Valorian leaders feared a world in which:

> **Kaltheim emerged from the war as the sole victorious superpower.**

Valoria therefore committed enormous resources to the war effort.

This marked the beginning of the modern alliance.

---

# The Uneasy Alliance

The Kaltheim-Valoria alliance was effective.

It was also:

> deeply uncomfortable.

The two nations differed on almost everything:

| Kaltheim | Valoria |
|-----------|-----------|
| Traditional | Modern |
| Religious | Secular |
| Collectivist | Individualist |
| Disciplined | Informal |
| Suspicious of Arcane Magic | Enthusiastic about Innovation |

Many Kaltheimians privately regarded the Velorians as:

> loud, undisciplined, materialistic, and spiritually adrift.

Many Velorians regarded Kaltheim as:

> humorless, authoritarian, secretive, and stubborn.

Neither side entirely disagreed.

---

# No Technology Sharing

Despite fighting on the same side:

> **there was almost no meaningful technological exchange.**

Kaltheim guarded its military and industrial secrets obsessively.

Examples included:

- aircraft technology,
- advanced metallurgy,
- armored doctrine,
- weapons development.

Valoria repeatedly requested access.

Kaltheim repeatedly refused.

Officially:

> for security reasons.

Unofficially:

> because Kaltheim did not trust Valoria.

---

# The OTS Incident

Near the end of the war, Kaltheim uncovered evidence that:

> **Valorian intelligence assets had been conducting espionage operations inside Kaltheim.**

The revelation nearly shattered the alliance.

The organization responsible would eventually evolve into the modern:

> **Office of Theoretical Security (OTS).**

The discovery confirmed many Kaltheimians' worst suspicions about Valoria.

The Velorians, meanwhile, argued:

> they merely sought to ensure the balance of power.

Kaltheim remained unconvinced.

---

# The End of the War

The Grand Alliance ultimately defeated the Imperial League.

Victory celebrations were held across Eismark.

Privately, many leaders worried that:

> the alliance would not survive peace.

They were almost correct.

---

# Modern Relations

Today, Kaltheim and Valoria remain:

> allies.

Both nations recognize that cooperation is necessary.

However:

> neither fully trusts the other.

Valoria continues attempting to:

- improve relations,
- increase cooperation,
- gain access to Kaltheimian technology.

Kaltheim continues politely refusing.

A common Kaltheimian joke goes:

> *"The Velorians are our closest allies. Creator willing, they shall remain at a safe distance."*

Meanwhile, a common Velorian joke is:

> *"One day Kaltheim will learn to have fun. We're determined to help."*

Neither nation finds the other's joke particularly amusing.

---

This relationship is honestly fantastic because it creates:

- political tension,
- espionage plots,
- diplomatic drama,
- military cooperation,
- and genuine mutual respect beneath all the suspicion.

It's one of the strongest geopolitical relationships in Eismark.
