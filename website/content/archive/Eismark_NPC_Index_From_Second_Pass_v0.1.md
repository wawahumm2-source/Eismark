Purpose: This file lists named NPCs, major figures, divine persons, and notable named ancestors/mentioned rulers found in the second-pass idea document. It is not a canon ruling; it is a review index.

## Primary Campaign NPCs

### Uriel

Aliases and Titles: Uriel the Rememberer; Uriel, Angel of Memory; the Departed Uriel; the Sleeper.

Role: First One tied to memory, truth, language, preservation, the Communion, the Great Forgetting, the Abandoned One, and the Bartender.

Notes: The document repeatedly treats Uriel as one of the central figures of the campaign. Several ideas describe him as fractured, dormant, separated from the Communion, and tragically connected to the Entity.

### The Abandoned One

Role: Uriel's Joivian form / the surviving Joivian that did not march into the Great Maw.

Notes: The document treats the Abandoned One as Uriel-related and central to the campaign mystery. Some entries state directly that "The Abandoned One is Uriel."

### The Bartender

Aliases and Titles: Keeper of The Bar; surviving fragment of Uriel; fragment of the First One Uriel.

Role: Metaphysical NPC tied to The Bar, memory, the Communion, grief, refuge, and character reflection.

Notes: The document gives the Bartender the most repeated NPC treatment. Suggested traits include kind, tired, patient, quietly humorous, melancholy, incomplete, unable to knowingly lie, unable or unwilling to give direct answers, and appearing sparingly at major story beats.

### Brother Remembrance

Aliases and Titles: Remembrance the Unyielding.

Role: Retired Exalted monk associated with the Monastery of Saint Uriel.

Notes: Presented as an important spiritual figure, especially among Automatons. He represents life after war and the possibility that beings built for violence can find peace.

### Magister Azhul the Dreaming King

Aliases and Titles: Azhul; Archmagister Severian Holt; the Dreaming King; Prophet of the New Flesh; the First Dreamer; the Sleeper Beneath the Hills; He Who Dreams in Color.

Role: Major villain or cosmic-horror antagonist.

Notes: Once a scholar named Severian Holt, he became a lich after pursuing truths connected to the Great Forgetting, the First Age, the Blood, or the Entity. His followers are the Dreamers of Azhul. The document repeatedly stresses that Azhul wants to meet Uriel and believes Uriel possesses the Final Truth.

### Professor Kathargeryhavander

Aliases and Titles: Professor Kath; Old Kath; the Last Wayfinder; Cartographer of the Shattered Isles; the Man Who Walked Beyond the Map; Professor Emeritus of Browning University.

Role: Dragonborn explorer, archaeologist, cartographer, historian, and senior fellow of the Commonwealth Geographical Society.

Notes: Presented as a beloved adventure-facing NPC. He is curious, kind, absent-minded, stubborn, and famous for discovering ruins and concealing at least one terrible discovery. He has worked with Uriel's Testament but opposes reckless scholarship.

### Director Marcus Vale

Role: Director of the Office of Tactical Security.

Notes: A major Velorian intelligence figure. The document frames him as patriotic, pragmatic, and willing to use covert methods to protect the Commonwealth. He is listed among the most important NPCs in one ranking.

### Father Lucian Vale

Aliases and Titles: Archpriest Lucian Vale of Sanctspire; the Pilgrim Saint; the Wandering Father.

Role: Kaltheimian priest, former archpriest, wandering religious figure.

Notes: Presented as a compassionate, disruptive, possibly saintly figure who left institutional power after war changed him. The document suggests he may be devoted to Seraphael.

## Political and Institutional Figures

### Archon Quintus Michael

Role: High-ranking Kaltheimian religious authority.

Notes: Mentioned as one of the most important NPCs and as someone who has never publicly commented on Father Lucian Vale.

### Queen Aelirya Silverbough

Aliases and Titles: Queen Aelirya.

Role: Ruler of the Sylvan Veil.

Notes: Mentioned as quietly supporting Uriel's Testament and as a ruler/person entry in the geopolitical material.

### King Alaric IV of House Vossen

Role: Placeholder monarch of Voss.

Notes: Mentioned under the Kingdom of Voss as a possible constitutional monarch. The document explicitly marks the name as a placeholder if changed later.

## First Ones and Divine Patron Figures

These figures function more like divine persons than ordinary NPCs, but they are named personalities and may matter in lore, visions, theology, or campaign scenes.

### Michael

Role: First One associated with protection, divine war, duty, sacrifice, and humanity.

Notes: Also appears indirectly through Archon Quintus Michael's name and Kaltheimian doctrine.

### Gabriel

Role: First One associated with understanding, mathematics, astronomy, philosophy, and dangerous scholarship.

### Luke

Role: First One associated with methodology, engineering, science, medicine, order, and systems.

### John

Role: First One associated with creativity, art, music, architecture, and invention.

### Raphael

Role: First One associated with healing, biology, agriculture, and mercy.

### Leonardo

Role: First One associated with craftsmanship, invention, smithing, and engineering.

Notes: Mentioned as a patron claimed by both Dwarves and Gnomes, with a running theological/cultural dispute.

### Asterion

Role: First One / patron associated with Giants.

Aliases and Titles: the Stonefather.

### Drakharion

Role: First One / patron associated with Dragonborn and dragons.

Aliases and Titles: the Eternal Patriarch; First Ancestor.

### Sylvara

Role: First One / patron associated with Elves and nature.

Aliases and Titles: the Green Mother.

### Callidora

Role: First One or revered divine figure associated with Elven devotion.

Notes: Mentioned as strongly revered by Elves alongside Sylvara and Uriel.

### Seraphael

Role: First One or divine patron associated with home, family, community, hospitality, and Halflings.

Aliases and Titles: the Mother.

Notes: The document suggests Father Lucian Vale is likely devoted to Seraphael.
